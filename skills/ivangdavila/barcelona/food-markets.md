# Food Markets — Barcelona

## Major Markets

### Mercat de la Boqueria (Sant Josep)

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Location** | Las Ramblas, 91 |
| **Hours** | Mon-Sat 8am-8:30pm |
| **Vibe** | Iconic but VERY touristy |
| **Price** | €€€ (tourist markup on prepared food) |

**Honest take:** Beautiful, iconic, must-see once. But front stalls are tourist traps charging €5 for a fruit cup. Go deep inside for real vendors and better prices.

**Best approach:**
- Go before 10am (locals shopping)
- Walk past front stalls (tourist zone)
- Eat at El Quim or Pinotxo (iconic bars inside)
- Buy groceries from back stalls

**Must-try inside:**
| Stall | What |
|-------|------|
| **El Quim de la Boqueria** | Eggs, seafood, excellent |
| **Bar Pinotxo** | Classic, Juanito is a legend |
| **Petràs** | Mushrooms (bolets) |

**Avoid:** Pre-cut fruit cups (overpriced), front stall juices (tourist prices)

### Mercat de Santa Caterina

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **Location** | Avinguda Francesc Cambó, 16 (El Born) |
| **Hours** | Mon-Sat 7:30am-3:30pm (Thu/Fri until 8:30pm) |
| **Vibe** | Local, less crowded than Boqueria |
| **Price** | €€ |

**Better than Boqueria for:** Actually shopping, less tourist chaos, beautiful architecture (Miralles roof).

**Must-try:**
- Fresh produce (cheaper than Boqueria)
- Tapas bars inside
- Restaurant Cuines Santa Caterina

**Best for:** Combining with El Born neighborhood exploration

## Market Strategy

### For Tourists (Short Visit)

| Priority | Market | Why |
|----------|--------|-----|
| Must-do | La Boqueria | Iconic (go early, go deep) |
| Better experience | Santa Caterina | Less crowded, beautiful |
| Sunday special | Sant Antoni | Book/vintage market |
| Skip | Random neighborhood markets | Unless you want authentic |

### For Residents

| Need | Market |
|------|--------|
| **Best quality** | Boqueria (back stalls), Santa Caterina |
| **Best value** | Neighborhood markets (Abaceria, Ninot) |
| **Food stall eating** | Boqueria (El Quim), Sant Antoni |
| **Flowers** | Concepció |
| **Sunday ritual** | Sant Antoni |

## Market Hours (Important!)

Most markets follow traditional Spanish hours:
- **Morning:** 8am-2pm/3pm
- **Closed:** Afternoon siesta (many)
- **Some evenings:** 5pm-8pm (check specific market)
- **Sunday:** CLOSED (except special markets like Sant Antoni outdoor)

**Monday warning:** Some stalls closed Mondays.
