# Uptown Barcelona — Eixample & Gràcia

## 1. Eixample Dreta (Right Eixample)

**Best for**: Professionals, Gaudí lovers, shopping enthusiasts, LGBTQ+ community

**Character**: Barcelona's most elegant neighborhood. The famous grid layout with chamfered corners. Passeig de Gràcia shopping, Casa Batlló, La Pedrera. Expensive but beautiful.

| Rent | 1BR: €1,500–2,000 | 2BR: €2,000–2,800 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Most beautiful architecture, Gaudí buildings, excellent shopping, very safe, central
**Cons**: Expensive, can feel commercial, less neighborhood feel, tourist traffic on Passeig de Gràcia

**Transit**: Passeig de Gràcia (L2, L3, L4), Diagonal (L3, L5), Verdaguer (L4, L5)
**Must-do**: Evening walk on Passeig de Gràcia, Casa Batlló illuminated, brunch at Federal

### Sub-areas:
- **Passeig de Gràcia corridor**: Most prestigious, expensive
- **Sagrada Família area**: More affordable, residential
- **Dreta interior**: Quieter, residential feel

---

## 2. Eixample Esquerra (Left Eixample)

**Best for**: Professionals, couples, those wanting Eixample quality at (slightly) lower prices

**Character**: Left side of Eixample. More residential than Dreta, excellent restaurants, Hospital Clínic area. Same grid beauty, less tourist focus.

| Rent | 1BR: €1,400–1,900 | 2BR: €1,800–2,500 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: More residential, great restaurants, slightly cheaper than Dreta, excellent transit
**Cons**: Less "iconic" than Dreta, can feel generic in parts

**Transit**: Hospital Clínic (L5), Entença (L5), Rocafort (L1)
**Must-do**: Mercat del Ninot, dinner in the neighborhood

---

## 3. Sant Antoni

**Best for**: Hipsters, foodies, young professionals, those wanting trendy but affordable

**Character**: Barcelona's most transformed neighborhood. Once working-class, now trendy epicenter. Mercat de Sant Antoni renovation brought new energy. Excellent restaurants, vintage shops.

| Rent | 1BR: €1,300–1,700 | 2BR: €1,600–2,200 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Trendy food scene, improving rapidly, excellent market, good metro, still some deals
**Cons**: Gentrifying fast (prices rising), can feel overly hipster, some grittier blocks remain

**Transit**: Sant Antoni (L2), Poble Sec (L3), Urgell (L1)
**Must-do**: Mercat de Sant Antoni (especially Sunday book market), Carrer del Parlament restaurants

### Sunday market:
The book/vintage market outside Mercat de Sant Antoni on Sundays is iconic. Arrive 10am.

---

## 4. Gràcia

**Best for**: Creatives, young families, those wanting village feel, bohemian types

**Character**: Barcelona's most beloved neighborhood. Former independent town, still feels like a village. Plazas (especially Plaça del Sol), independent shops, excellent restaurants. Strong local identity.

| Rent | 1BR: €1,300–1,800 | 2BR: €1,700–2,400 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Best neighborhood character, village atmosphere, excellent plazas, local feel, safe
**Cons**: Hills, further from beach, some streets narrow/noisy, popular = competitive rentals

**Transit**: Fontana (L3), Diagonal (L3, L5), Lesseps (L3)
**Must-do**: Evening at Plaça del Sol, Festa Major de Gràcia (August), Sunday vermut

### Key plazas:
- **Plaça del Sol**: Most popular, lively evenings
- **Plaça de la Virreina**: Quieter, families
- **Plaça del Diamant**: Literary fame (Rodoreda novel)
- **Plaça de la Vila de Gràcia**: Historic center

### August warning:
Festa Major de Gràcia (mid-August) = streets decorated, week of parties. Amazing if visiting, chaotic if living there.

## 6. Sarrià-Sant Gervasi

**Best for**: Wealthy families, expats with children, those wanting international school access

**Character**: Barcelona's most exclusive area. Large houses, international schools, quiet streets. Upper Barcelona with views. Very different from rest of city.

| Rent | 1BR: €1,800–2,500 | 2BR: €2,500–4,000+ |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Safest area, best schools (international), space, quiet, green areas, Tibidabo nearby
**Cons**: Very expensive, feels suburban, disconnected from city life, car sometimes needed

**Transit**: FGC (Sarrià, Tres Torres, Sant Gervasi), limited metro
**Must-do**: Tibidabo views, old Sarrià village

### Sub-areas:
- **Sarrià**: Village center, charming, most expensive
- **Sant Gervasi**: More urban, still upscale
- **Bonanova**: Residential, family-focused
- **Vallvidrera/Tibidabo**: Mountain, views, isolated

---

## 7. Pedralbes

**Best for**: Ultra-wealthy, those near Zona Universitària, seeking maximum space

**Character**: Barcelona's equivalent of Beverly Hills. Large villas, embassies, monasteries. Very quiet.

| Rent | 1BR: €2,000+ | 2BR: €3,000+ (houses €5,000+) |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros**: Extremely safe, spacious, prestige, Pedralbes Monastery gardens
**Cons**: Isolated, needs car, no nightlife, very expensive

**Transit**: Zona Universitària (L3), Palau Reial (L3)

## Uptown Summary

### Profile Matching:

| Profile | Best Choice | Why |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Young professional | Gràcia, Sant Antoni | Social, character |
| Couple (budget) | Eixample Esquerra | Quality, value |
| Couple (splurge) | Eixample Dreta | Beautiful, central |
| Family (local school) | Gràcia | Village, community |
| Family (international school) | Sarrià | Schools, safe |
| Creative | Gràcia, Sant Antoni | Vibe |
| LGBTQ+ | Eixample ("Gaixample") | Community, bars |

### "Gaixample"
The area around Consell de Cent / Muntaner in Eixample Esquerra is Barcelona's gay neighborhood. Bars, clubs, restaurants catering to LGBTQ+ community. Very welcoming.
