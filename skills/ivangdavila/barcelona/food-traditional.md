# Traditional Catalan Food — Barcelona

## Pa amb Tomàquet (Pan con Tomate)

THE essential Catalan dish. Bread rubbed with ripe tomato, drizzled with olive oil, salt. Served with everything. Simple perfection.

| Where | Location | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| **Everywhere** | All of Barcelona | Every restaurant has it |
| **Cal Pep** | El Born | Excellent version |
| **Can Culleretes** | Gòtic | Traditional |

**How to eat:** Made at table — you rub tomato on bread yourself, or it comes prepared.

**Tips:**
- Quality depends on: bread (pa de pagès), tomatoes (must be ripe), oil (good EVOO)
- Available year-round but best in tomato season (summer)
- Often comes with cold cuts or as side

## Calçots (Seasonal: Jan-Mar)

Long spring onions, charred over open fire, dipped in romesco sauce. Messy, communal, essential Catalan winter experience.

| Where | When | Notes |
|-------|------|-------|
| **Calçotades** | January-March | Outdoor events, often rural |
| **Restaurants** | Winter season | Seasonal menus |
| **Day trips** | Valls area | Calçot capital |

**How to eat:**
1. Peel off charred outer layer
2. Hold by green end, dip in romesco
3. Lower into mouth, bite, discard green
4. Wear a bib — it's messy

**Best experience:** Day trip to Valls (1hr from Barcelona) for authentic calçotada.

## Arròs a la Cassola / Paella Variants

Rice dishes are Valencian origin but Barcelona does them excellently. Key: cooked in wide shallow pan (cassola).

### Types

| Dish | What | Where |
|------|------|-------|
| **Paella** | Saffron rice, traditionally rabbit/chicken | Can Ros, La Mar Salada |
| **Arròs Negre** | Black rice (squid ink) | Barceloneta |
| **Fideuà** | Noodles instead of rice | Barceloneta, Poblenou |
| **Arròs a Banda** | Rice cooked in fish broth | Barceloneta |

### Best Places

| Restaurant | Location | Price | Notes |
|------------|----------|-------|-------|
| **Can Ros** | Barceloneta | €20-25/person | Local favorite |
| **La Mar Salada** | Barceloneta | €18-24 | Good quality |
| **Elche** | Vila Olímpica | €22-28 | Excellent rice |
| **Barraca** | Barceloneta | €20-26 | Beach views |

### AVOID
- **Beachfront restaurants on paseo** — Tourist traps, mediocre, overpriced
- **Anywhere with photos of paella in window** — Red flag

**Tip:** Good paella takes 20-30 min to cook. If it arrives in 5 min, it was pre-made.

## Coca (Catalan Flatbread)

Flat bread with various toppings — savory (vegetables, sausage) or sweet (candied fruit).

**Types:**
- **Coca de recapte**: Vegetables, traditional
- **Coca de Sant Joan**: Sweet, for June 23 celebration
- **Coca de llardons**: With pork cracklings

**Where:** Bakeries, traditional restaurants

## Where NOT to Eat Traditional Food

| Avoid | Why |
|-------|-----|
| **Las Ramblas restaurants** | Tourist trap, 2-3x markup |
| **Barceloneta beachfront first line** | Mediocre paella, overpriced |
| **Anywhere with "tourist menu"** | Designed to extract money |
| **Photos of food in window** | Usually bad sign |

**Instead:** Walk 2-3 blocks from any tourist spot. Quality up, prices down.
