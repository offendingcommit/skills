# Tech Industry Guide — Barcelona

## Tech Scene Overview

Barcelona is Southern Europe's largest tech hub and Spain's startup capital. The 22@ district is Europe's most successful urban tech transformation. Strong in mobile, gaming, e-commerce, and travel tech. Mobile World Congress brings global attention annually.

**Key stats (2025):**
- 2,285+ active startups in Catalonia
- 1,428 tech companies in Barcelona proper
- 27,000+ tech jobs
- 200+ multinational tech hubs
- #1 in Southern Europe for VC investment
- Host of Mobile World Congress

## Visa for Tech Workers

### EU Citizens
No visa needed. Work freely.

### Non-EU Citizens

#### Digital Nomad Visa (2023+)

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **For** | Remote workers for non-Spanish companies |
| **Income** | Min €2,520/month |
| **Duration** | 1 year, renewable to 5 years |
| **Tax** | Special regime possible (24% flat for 5 years) |
| **Processing** | 2-4 weeks |

#### Highly Qualified Professional Visa

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **For** | Employed by Spanish company |
| **Salary threshold** | €42,000/year |
| **Education** | University degree usually required |
| **Sponsorship** | Employer must apply |
| **Timeline** | 4-8 weeks |

## Tax Considerations

### Beckham Law (Flat Tax)

If you qualify:
- 24% flat tax on Spanish income (vs progressive up to 47%)
- 6-year eligibility
- For those who haven't been Spanish tax resident in 10 years
- Also applies to digital nomad visa holders

### Regular Tax Brackets (2026)

| Income | Marginal Rate |
|--------|---------------|
| Up to €12,450 | 19% |
| €12,450-20,200 | 24% |
| €20,200-35,200 | 30% |
| €35,200-60,000 | 37% |
| €60,000-300,000 | 45% |
| Over €300,000 | 47% |

**Note:** Catalonia has slightly higher regional rates than Madrid.
