# South SF Neighborhoods

## Mission District

**Best for:** Young professionals, nightlife, food lovers

**Character:** Vibrant Latino culture mixed with tech gentrification. Best Mexican food in SF. Street art, murals, trendy bars.

| Rent | 1BR: $3,000-3,500 | 2BR: $4,000-5,000 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros:** Sunniest neighborhood, amazing food, nightlife, walkable
**Cons:** Gentrifying, some homeless presence, car break-ins

**Transit:** BART (16th & 24th St), MUNI buses
**Must-do:** Dolores Park, Valencia Street, taquerias, Balmy Alley murals

---

## Castro

**Best for:** LGBTQ+ community, history buffs, community-oriented

**Character:** Historic LGBTQ+ neighborhood, rainbow crosswalks, strong community. Active nightlife.

| Rent | 1BR: $2,800-3,400 | 2BR: $3,800-4,800 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros:** Safe, sunny, strong community, good transit
**Cons:** Hilly, parking tough, some tourist crowds

**Transit:** MUNI Metro (Castro Station)
**Must-do:** Castro Theatre, Twin Peaks views, Harvey Milk Plaza

---

## Noe Valley

**Best for:** Families, quiet living, stroller-friendly

**Character:** Suburban feel in the city. Quiet streets, boutique shops, families everywhere.

| Rent | 1BR: $3,500-4,500 | 2BR: $5,000-6,500 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros:** Very safe, quiet, sunny, family-friendly
**Cons:** Expensive, quiet (boring for some), limited nightlife

**Transit:** J-Church MUNI, buses
**Must-do:** 24th Street shopping, Noe Valley Farmers Market

---

## Potrero Hill

**Best for:** Tech workers, sunny weather lovers, dog owners

**Character:** Sunny, residential, near Mission Bay tech campuses. Good views, quiet.

| Rent | 1BR: $2,800-3,400 | 2BR: $3,800-5,000 |
|------|-------------------|-------------------|

**Pros:** Sunniest in SF, safe, near tech offices, views
**Cons:** Hilly, limited nightlife, fewer restaurants

**Transit:** Buses, MUNI T-line
**Must-do:** 18th Street restaurants, McKinley Square views
