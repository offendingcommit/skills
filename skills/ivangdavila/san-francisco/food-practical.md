# Food Practical Guide — San Francisco

## Coffee Culture

SF is a third-wave coffee capital.

### Local Roasters

| Roaster | Flagship | Notes |
|---------|----------|-------|
| **Sightglass** | SoMa | Industrial space |
| **Four Barrel** | Mission | Hipster classic |
| **Ritual** | Mission | OG third wave |
| **Blue Bottle** | Multiple | Started in Oakland |
| **Equator** | Fort Mason | Best views |
| **Andytown** | Sunset | Snowy Plover (Irish coffee riff) |

### Classic Spots
- **Caffe Trieste** — North Beach institution
- **Philz** — SF-born, personalized
- **Peet's** — Bay Area original

## Reservations

### Hard-to-Book
- **State Bird Provisions** — Book exactly 60 days out
- **Lazy Bear** — Tickets sell out fast
- **House of Prime Rib** — Call ahead
- **Swan Oyster Depot** — No reservations, arrive early

### Apps
- **Resy** — Most upscale restaurants
- **OpenTable** — Broader coverage
- **Tock** — Tasting menus

---

| App | Notes |

## Tipping

| Where | How Much |
|-------|----------|
| **Restaurants** | 18-20% |
| **Fine dining** | 20-22% |
| **Counter service** | $1-2 or 10% |

## Money-Saving

| Tip | Details |
|-----|---------|
| **Mission burritos** | $12-15 for huge meal |
| **Chinatown** | $8-12 lunches |
| **Farmers markets** | Good lunch options |
| **Happy hours** | Many restaurants 4-6pm |
| **Lunch specials** | Fine dining often has cheaper lunch |
| **Grocery stores** | Trader Joe's prepared foods |
