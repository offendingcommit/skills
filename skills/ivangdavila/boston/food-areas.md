## South End

### Why It's Best
Boston's undisputed dining capital. Chef-driven restaurants, diverse cuisines, walkable concentration.

### The Vibe
Victorian brownstones, outdoor patios, LGBTQ-friendly, upscale-casual mix.

### Top Restaurants

| Restaurant | Cuisine | Price | Notes |
|------------|---------|-------|-------|
| Toro | Spanish tapas | $$$ | Ken Oringer, always packed |
| Myers + Chang | Asian fusion | $$$ | Joanne Chang, inventive |
| B&G Oysters | Seafood | $$$ | Barbara Lynch, raw bar |
| Coppa | Italian | $$$ | House-made pasta |
| The Gallows | American | $$ | Cocktails, brunch |
| SRV | Venetian | $$$ | Pasta, wine |

### Best Streets
- Tremont Street: Main restaurant row
- Washington Street: More casual, diverse
- Shawmut Ave: Neighborhood gems

### Tips
- Reservations recommended weekends
- Walk around, many options
- Brunch scene is strong

## Cambridge (Harvard/Central)

### Why It's Good
Academic influence brings diverse, interesting food. More casual than South End.

### The Vibe
Students, professors, tech workers. Ranges from cheap eats to innovative fine dining.

### Top Restaurants

| Restaurant | Cuisine | Price | Notes |
|------------|---------|-------|-------|
| Oleana | Mediterranean | $$$ | Ana Sortun, legendary |
| Alden & Harlow | American | $$$ | Creative, cocktails |
| Area Four | Pizza, American | $$ | Cambridge favorite |
| Giulia | Italian | $$$ | Pasta destination |
| Life Alive | Vegetarian | $ | Healthy, energizing |

### Best Areas
- Harvard Square: Tourist-friendly, some gems
- Central Square: Grittier, more diverse, better value
- Inman Square: Locals' favorite, residential

### Tips
- Central Square has better value
- Walk between squares (15-20 min)
- Ethnic food strongest in Central

## North End

### Why It's Essential
Authentic Italian-American tradition. History. The pizza.

### The Vibe
Narrow streets, old-school, tourists and locals mix.

### Top Restaurants

| Restaurant | Cuisine | Price | Notes |
|------------|---------|-------|-------|
| Neptune Oyster | Seafood | $$$ | Lobster roll legend |
| Regina Pizzeria | Pizza | $ | Cash only, worth it |
| Pomodoro | Italian | $$$ | Tiny, romantic |
| Daily Catch | Seafood/Pasta | $$ | Squid ink, tiny |

### Tips
- Avoid obvious tourist traps (bright lights, hawkers)
- Cash for many places
- Walk side streets for hidden gems

## Somerville (Union/Davis)

### Why It's Hot
Emerging food scene, chefs priced out of Boston, authenticity.

### The Vibe
Hip, diverse, young. Feels less polished, more interesting.

### Top Restaurants

| Restaurant | Cuisine | Price | Notes |
|------------|---------|-------|-------|
| Juliet | American | $$$ | Small, seasonal |
| Sarma | Mediterranean | $$$ | Same chef as Oleana |
| Bow Market | Various | $-$$ | Food hall complex |
| Celeste | Peruvian | $$ | Neighborhood gem |

### Best Areas
- Union Square: Hottest restaurants
- Davis Square: More established, bars
- Bow Market: Shipping containers, trendy

### Tips
- Green Line extension makes access easier
- Less formal than Boston
- Some cash-only spots

## Chinatown

### Why It's Essential
Authentic Chinese, late-night options, cheap.

### The Vibe
Dense, busy, authentic. Language barriers sometimes.

### Top Restaurants
(See `food-ethnic.md` for details)
- Gourmet Dumpling House
- China Pearl (dim sum)
- Peach Farm

### Tips

- Cash preferred at many
