## Must-See (Worth the Hype)

### Freedom Trail
2.5-mile red brick line connecting 16 Revolutionary War sites.

**Worth it for:** Boston Common, Old North Church, Paul Revere House, Faneuil Hall
**Skip:** The paid tours unless you love detailed history. Self-guided is fine.
**Time:** 2-3 hours walking, full day if entering all sites
**Cost:** Trail is free. Individual sites $5-15 each.

### Fenway Park
America's oldest MLB stadium (1912). Intimate, historic atmosphere.

**Do:** Tour even if no game ($25). See Pesky's Pole, Green Monster, manual scoreboard.
**Game day:** Bleacher seats $35-60 for experience without premium price.
**Skip:** Standing room only unless desperate.
**Tip:** Fenway Franks are mandatory.

### Harvard Yard
Walk through America's oldest university campus (1636).

**Do:** Free self-guided tour, touch John Harvard statue (tourist ritual, everyone does it).
**Skip:** Paid "Harvard student" tours from random people outside gates.
**Time:** 1-2 hours with Harvard Square browsing.
**Tip:** The Harvard Coop bookstore has good merch.

### MIT Campus
Walk the Infinite Corridor, see the Stata Center's wild architecture.

**Do:** Self-guided architecture tour. The Ray and Maria Stata Center is Instagram gold.
**Skip:** MIT Museum unless very interested in science history.
**Free:** Hacks (student pranks) are legendary. Look for unexpected objects on domes.

### Boston Public Library
America's first large free public library. The McKim Building is architectural masterpiece.

**Do:** See the courtyard, Bates Hall reading room, John Singer Sargent murals.
**Free:** Always. Just walk in.
**Time:** 45 minutes.

### North End
Boston's Little Italy. Dense, historic, incredible food.

**Do:** Walk Hanover Street, eat at Regina Pizzeria or Modern Pastry.
**Skip:** Tourist trap restaurants on main drag. Ask locals.
**Tip:** Cash only at many iconic spots.

### Boston Harbor Islands
National park accessible by ferry from downtown.

**Do:** Spectacle Island (beaches, views), Georges Island (Civil War fort).
**Time:** Half day minimum.
**Season:** May-October ferry service.

## Good But Not Essential

### New England Aquarium
Solid aquarium, giant ocean tank centerpiece.

**Worth it for:** Families, rainy days, penguin viewing
**Skip if:** You've seen major aquariums elsewhere
**Cost:** $35 adults
**Tip:** IMAX separate ticket, often skippable

### Museum of Fine Arts
World-class collection, massive building.

**Worth it for:** Art lovers, Impressionist collection, Egyptian wing
**Skip if:** Limited time, prefer history
**Cost:** $27 adults, free Wednesday evenings after 4pm
**Time:** 3+ hours to see highlights

### Isabella Stewart Gardner Museum
Venetian-style palace with courtyard, famous art heist story.

**Worth it for:** The building itself, courtyard garden, unique collection
**Skip if:** Only interested in traditional museum experience
**Cost:** $20. Free if your name is Isabella.

### USS Constitution
Oldest commissioned warship still afloat (1797).

**Worth it for:** Naval history buffs, free, unique experience
**Skip if:** Not into military history
**Where:** Charlestown Navy Yard
**Free:** Always, run by US Navy

### Boston Common and Public Garden
America's oldest park (1634) connected to Victorian Public Garden.

**Do:** Swan Boats (kitschy but iconic), Make Way for Ducklings statues
**Time:** 30-60 minutes strolling
**Free:** Always (Swan Boats $4.50)

### Faneuil Hall and Quincy Market
Historic meeting hall plus food hall complex.

**Reality:** Very touristy but decent food court options.
**Do:** Walk through, grab a quick bite if hungry.
**Skip:** Expensive restaurants here. Better options nearby.

## Skip These

### Plymouth Rock
Just a rock with "1620" carved on it. 45 minutes from Boston. Underwhelming.

**Instead:** If you want Pilgrim history, Plimoth Patuxet Museums is better.

### Duck Tours
Overpriced amphibious vehicles with corny narration.

**Reality:** $55 for what you can see walking for free.
**Instead:** Walk the Freedom Trail, take harbor ferry.

### Salem (Most of It)
Witch trial kitsch dominates. Very commercial.

**Exception:** Peabody Essex Museum is legitimately world-class.
**If you go:** Halloween season is mobbed. Off-season quieter.

### Cheers Bar
The TV show was filmed in LA. This is just a bar with merchandise.

**Reality:** Generic bar trading on 1980s sitcom nostalgia.
**Instead:** Literally any actual Boston bar.

### TD Garden Tours
Unless you're seeing a game, it's just an arena.

**Instead:** Fenway Park tour is 100x better.
