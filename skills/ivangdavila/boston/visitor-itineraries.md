# Visitor Itineraries — Boston

## 1 Day: Greatest Hits

Best for: First-time visitors with limited time

### Morning (9am-12pm)
1. **Start at Boston Common** (30 min)
   - Walk through America's oldest public park
   - See Frog Pond, State House golden dome views
   
2. **Freedom Trail to North End** (2 hours)
   - Follow red brick line through downtown
   - Key stops: Old South Meeting House, Old North Church
   - End at Paul Revere House

### Lunch (12-1pm)
**North End options:**
- Regina Pizzeria (cash only, worth the line)
- Neptune Oyster (upscale, expect wait)
- Giacomo's (cheap, huge portions, cash only)

### Afternoon (1-5pm)
3. **Quincy Market walkthrough** (30 min)
   - Quick loop, food court options
   
4. **Walk to Copley Square** (take T if tired)
   - Boston Public Library (45 min)
   - Trinity Church exterior
   
5. **Back Bay stroll**
   - Newbury Street shopping/browsing
   - Commonwealth Avenue mall

### Evening
- Dinner in South End or Back Bay
- Optional: Red Sox game if in season

## 2 Days: Add Cambridge

### Day 1: Boston Core
Follow 1-day itinerary above

### Day 2: Cambridge + More Boston

**Morning (9am-12pm)**
1. **Take Red Line to Harvard** (30 min total)
2. **Harvard Yard walking tour** (1.5 hours)
   - Harvard Square browsing
   - Touch John Harvard statue
   - Harvard Coop bookstore

3. **Walk to MIT** (20 min along Charles River)
   - Or take bus 1 down Mass Ave
   
**Lunch in Cambridge**
- Cambridge-1 (pizza)
- Legal Sea Foods (classic)
- Veggie Galaxy (vegetarian diner)

**Afternoon**
4. **MIT Campus** (1 hour)
   - Stata Center architecture
   - Infinite Corridor
   - Look for hacks (pranks)

5. **Return to Boston**
   - Museum of Fine Arts (2-3 hours), OR
   - Fenway Park tour (1.5 hours)

**Evening**
- Dinner in Seaport or North End
- Drinks in Cambridge (Central Square) or South End

## Seasonal Adjustments

### Winter (Dec-Mar)
- Prioritize indoor attractions (museums, aquarium)
- Bundle up for outdoor walks — wind is brutal
- Ice skating at Frog Pond
- Holiday: Faneuil Hall tree, SoWa Winter Market

### Spring (Apr-May)
- Weather unpredictable — pack layers
- Boston Marathon (third Monday of April) — streets closed
- Cherry blossoms at Charles River Esplanade
- Red Sox season starts

### Summer (Jun-Aug)
- Harbor Islands open — add to any itinerary
- Outdoor concerts at Hatch Shell (free)
- Rooftop bars optimal
- AC is inconsistent in old buildings

### Fall (Sep-Nov)
- Best weather for walking
- Head of the Charles (rowing, October) brings crowds
- Foliage peaks mid-October
- College move-in crowds early September
