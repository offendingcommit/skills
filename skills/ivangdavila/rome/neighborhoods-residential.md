# Residential Neighborhoods

## Testaccio

### Overview
- **Vibe**: Working-class turned foodie haven, authentic
- **Rent 1BR**: EUR 900-1,300/month
- **Walk Score**: 85

### Who Lives Here
- Longtime Roman families
- Foodie expats
- Young professionals
- Creative types
- Restaurant industry workers

### Why It's Special
- Best food neighborhood in Rome (not even close)
- Former slaughterhouse district = offal tradition
- Testaccio Market (covered market, amazing)
- Real Roman neighborhood feel
- Monte Testaccio (ancient pottery hill)

### Pros
- Best food scene (carbonara, cacio e pepe)
- Authentic working-class character
- Near center (20 min walk)
- Metro access (Piramide)
- Good nightlife (clubs)
- Reasonable prices

### Cons
- Not "pretty" in classic Rome way
- Some areas industrial
- Nightclub noise (weekend nights)
- Limited housing stock

### Key Spots
- Testaccio Market (must visit)
- Monte Testaccio (pottery hill)
- MACRO museum (contemporary art)
- Pyramid of Cestius (Roman pyramid!)

### Best For
Foodies, authentic Rome seekers, young expats, creative professionals.

## San Giovanni

### Overview
- **Vibe**: Local working/middle class, practical
- **Rent 1BR**: EUR 800-1,200/month
- **Walk Score**: 80

### Who Lives Here
- Middle-class Romans
- Families
- Budget-conscious expats
- Office workers
- Students (some)

### Pros
- Excellent metro (Line A + C)
- Real local neighborhood
- Basilica di San Giovanni (major church)
- Reasonable prices
- Near Termini but better area
- Markets and local shops

### Cons
- Not charming or trendy
- Far from Trastevere/centro
- Can feel suburban
- Limited tourist appeal (pro and con)

### Key Spots
- San Giovanni in Laterano (basilica)
- Porta San Giovanni
- Via Appia starts nearby

### Best For
Budget-conscious expats, practical residents, families on budget.

## Esquilino / Piazza Vittorio

### Overview
- **Vibe**: Multicultural, diverse, gritty
- **Rent 1BR**: EUR 700-1,100/month
- **Walk Score**: 85

### Who Lives Here
- Immigrant communities (Chinese, Bengali, etc.)
- Budget travelers
- Students
- Some artists/creatives

### Pros
- Most diverse neighborhood
- Cheapest near center
- Excellent transport (Termini, Vittorio)
- International food
- Piazza Vittorio market

### Cons
- Can feel unsafe (perception > reality)
- Gritty aesthetics
- Not pretty
- Requires adaptation

### Reality Check
- Not dangerous, but not charming
- If you appreciate diversity, it's interesting
- If you want classic Rome, look elsewhere

### Best For
Budget priority, cultural diversity appreciators, adaptable expats.

## Ostiense

### Overview
- **Vibe**: Former industrial, emerging, nightlife
- **Rent 1BR**: EUR 800-1,200/month
- **Walk Score**: 70

### Who Lives Here
- Young professionals
- Creatives
- Students
- Early gentrifiers

### Transformation
- Former industrial/port area
- Now: clubs, restaurants, galleries
- Centrale Montemartini museum
- Street art scene (Ostiense district)

### Pros
- Nightlife scene
- Interesting architecture
- Street art
- Near Testaccio food
- More affordable

### Cons
- Still developing
- Some areas sketchy
- Far from centro
- Industrial aesthetics

### Best For
Young creatives, nightlife lovers, people seeking emerging areas.
