## System Overview

Italy has a **universal healthcare system** (Servizio Sanitario Nazionale - SSN). Quality is generally good, though bureaucracy and wait times can be challenging.

### Key Concepts

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| SSN | National Health Service (public) |
| ASL | Local Health Authority |
| Medico di base | General practitioner (GP) |
| Tessera sanitaria | Health card |
| Ticket | Co-payment for services |
| Ricetta | Prescription |

---

## Who Gets Coverage

### Automatic Coverage
- Italian citizens
- EU/EEA citizens with EHIC (temporary)
- Legal residents with employment
- Registered family members

### Registration Required
- Non-EU with Permesso di Soggiorno
- Students (with enrollment proof)
- Those with elective residence visa

### Not Covered (Need Private)
- Tourists (use travel insurance)
- Undocumented (emergency only)
- Short-term visitors

---

## Registering with SSN

### EU Citizens
1. Get Codice Fiscale
2. Go to local ASL
3. Register residency (or show EHIC for temporary)
4. Choose Medico di base (GP)
5. Receive Tessera sanitaria

### Non-EU with Permesso
1. Have valid Permesso di Soggiorno
2. Get Codice Fiscale
3. Go to local ASL
4. Pay annual contribution (~EUR 400 if not employed)
5. Choose Medico di base
6. Receive Tessera sanitaria

### Documents Needed
- Passport
- Codice Fiscale
- Permesso di Soggiorno (non-EU)
- Proof of residence
- Employment contract or student enrollment

---

## Finding a Doctor

### Medico di Base (GP)
- Your assigned primary doctor
- Choose from available list at ASL
- Free consultations
- Referrals to specialists
- Prescriptions
- Can change GP (process at ASL)

### How It Works
1. Call GP office for appointment
2. Some walk-in hours
3. Bring Tessera sanitaria
4. GP refers to specialists if needed
5. GP provides prescriptions (ricette)

### Choosing a GP
- Ask locals for recommendations
- Check if they speak English
- Location matters (near home)
- Availability (some very busy)

## Specialists & Hospitals

### Public System

**Referral Process**
1. GP provides referral (impegnativa)
2. Book via CUP (booking center)
3. Wait for appointment
4. Pay ticket (co-payment) at appointment

**Wait Times**
- Can be weeks to months
- "Urgente" referrals faster
- Private faster (if paying)

**Costs (Tickets)**
- Specialist visit: EUR 30-50
- Tests/diagnostics: varies
- Some categories exempt (income, age, conditions)

### Public Hospitals

**Major Hospitals in Rome**
- Policlinico Umberto I (largest, university)
- Policlinico Gemelli (Catholic University)
- San Camillo-Forlanini
- Sant'Andrea
- San Giovanni

**Emergency (Pronto Soccorso)**
- Free for emergencies
- Triage system (color codes)
- Can be very long waits
- Bring documents if possible

## Private Healthcare

### Why Go Private
- Faster appointments
- English-speaking doctors
- More modern facilities (sometimes)
- Choice of doctor
- Better amenities

### Costs (Without Insurance)
| Service | Cost Range |
|---------|------------|
| GP visit | EUR 80-150 |
| Specialist | EUR 100-250 |
| Basic blood tests | EUR 50-150 |
| MRI/CT scan | EUR 200-500 |
| ER visit | EUR 200-500 |
| Surgery | EUR 2,000-20,000+ |

### Private Hospitals

**International-Friendly**
- **Rome American Hospital**: English-speaking, expat-focused
- **Salvator Mundi International Hospital**: International staff
- **Villa Stuart**: Sports medicine, orthopedics

**Quality Private Italian**
- Policlinico Gemelli (also public)
- European Hospital
- Mater Dei

### Health Insurance

**Private Insurance Options**
- International plans (Cigna, Allianz, etc.)
- Italian insurers (Generali, UniSalute)
- Employer plans
- Cost: EUR 100-300/month depending on coverage

**What to Look For**
- English-speaking services
- Direct billing to hospitals
- Repatriation coverage
- Pre-existing conditions policy
- Maternity if needed

## Pharmacies (Farmacie)

### Finding One
- Green cross sign (lit when open)
- Very common throughout city
- Some 24-hour (turno)
- Check "farmacia di turno" for night/weekend

### Services
- Prescription filling
- Over-the-counter advice
- Minor ailment guidance
- Blood pressure checks
- Some basic tests

### Common Medications
- Many drugs prescription-only in Italy
- Bring supply of regular medications
- Bring prescription/letter from home doctor
- Generic equivalents available

### Costs
- Prescription drugs: subsidized with SSN
- OTC: full price
- Some brand preferences

### Not Covered by SSN
- Mostly private in Italy
- Basic extractions covered at ASL (limited)
- Most people pay out of pocket or have insurance

### Costs
| Service | Cost Range |
|---------|------------|

| Filling | EUR 80-150 |

- Ask for recommendations
- Check qualifications (Odontoiatra)

- Compare prices (varies widely)

## Emergency Situations

### Numbers
- **118**: Medical emergency
- **112**: General emergency

### Pronto Soccorso (ER)
- Go for genuine emergencies
- Triage by severity (white, green, yellow, red)
- White/green: very long waits
- Bring documents (passport, tessera sanitaria)
- Free for emergencies

### What Counts as Emergency
- Chest pain, difficulty breathing
- Severe bleeding
- Loss of consciousness
- Severe allergic reaction
- Serious trauma

### Non-Emergency
- Guardia Medica (night/weekend GP service)
- Call GP
- Pharmacy advice
- Private clinic

---

## Prescriptions

### How It Works
1. GP or specialist writes "ricetta"
2. Ricetta can be digital (dematerializzata)
3. Go to any pharmacy
4. Pharmacist fills prescription
5. Pay ticket (if applicable) or full price

### Types of Ricette
- **Ricetta bianca**: White, private prescription, you pay
- **Ricetta rossa/SSN**: Subsidized, pay ticket only
- **Ricetta ripetibile**: Can be used multiple times

### Controlled Medications
- Stricter rules
- May need special prescription
- Some medications unavailable
- Check before traveling with medications

---

## For Tourists

### Travel Insurance
- Essential
- Emergency coverage
- Repatriation
- Check policy details

### If You Get Sick
1. Minor: Pharmacy advice
2. Moderate: Private clinic or doctor
3. Emergency: Pronto Soccorso (ER)
4. Contact insurance for guidance

### EU Citizens
- EHIC covers emergency care
- May need to pay upfront, claim back
- Not a substitute for travel insurance

### Costs Without Insurance
- ER visit: EUR 200-500 minimum
- Hospital stay: EUR 500-1,500+/day
- Air ambulance: EUR 20,000-100,000

## Practical Tips

### Language
- Many doctors speak some English
- Medical terminology is Latin-based
- Bring translator if needed
- Private hospitals more English-friendly

### Documentation
- Keep all receipts
- Get medical reports in writing
- Useful for insurance claims
- Request English translation if needed

### Medications from Home
- Bring adequate supply
- Carry doctor's letter
- Check Italian equivalent names
- Some may not be available
