# Itineraries — Rome

## 1-Day Itinerary (First-Timer)

### Morning (8am-1pm)
1. **Vatican Museums & Sistine Chapel** (8:30am entry)
   - Book in advance, arrive at opening
   - 2.5-3 hours inside
   - End at Sistine Chapel

2. **St. Peter's Basilica** (11:30am)
   - Exit Sistine to basilica (if tour allows)
   - Otherwise walk around to entrance
   - 1 hour inside, optional dome climb

3. **Lunch in Prati** (1pm)
   - Cross the Tiber
   - Pizzarium (Bonci's legendary pizza)
   - Or any local trattoria

### Afternoon (2pm-6pm)
4. **Walk to Castel Sant'Angelo** (exterior)
   - Cross Ponte Sant'Angelo for photos
   - Beautiful bridge with Bernini angels

5. **Piazza Navona** (3pm)
   - Bernini's fountains
   - Coffee or gelato break
   - 30-45 minutes

6. **Pantheon** (4pm)
   - 30-45 minutes inside
   - Marvel at the dome

7. **Trevi Fountain** (5pm)
   - Throw a coin
   - Photos (it will be crowded)

### Evening (7pm-late)
8. **Aperitivo in Campo de' Fiori** (7pm)
   - Classic Italian tradition
   - Drink + snacks before dinner

9. **Dinner in Trastevere** (9pm)
   - Walk across the river
   - Da Enzo, Tonnarello, or wander
   - Stroll the neighborhood after

## 3-Day Itinerary (Comprehensive)

### Day 1: Vatican & Historic Center

**Morning:**
- Vatican Museums & Sistine Chapel (book 8:30am)
- St. Peter's Basilica (enter after museum)

**Lunch:**
- Prati neighborhood (Pizzarium or Sciascia)

**Afternoon:**
- Castel Sant'Angelo (optional interior)
- Walk to Piazza Navona
- Pantheon
- Walk to Trevi Fountain

**Evening:**
- Aperitivo near Pantheon
- Dinner in Campo de' Fiori or Jewish Ghetto

### Day 2: Ancient Rome

**Morning:**
- Colosseum (book 8:30am entry)
- Roman Forum & Palatine Hill

**Lunch:**
- Testaccio (Felice, Flavio al Velavevodetto)
- Best carbonara in Rome

**Afternoon:**
- Capitoline Museums (or skip if museum-fatigued)
- Walk the centro storico
- Spanish Steps (no sitting!)
- Via del Corso shopping

**Evening:**
- Sunset at Pincian Hill (above Spanish Steps)
- Dinner in centro or Trastevere

### Day 3: Neighborhoods & Local Life

**Morning:**
- Trastevere morning walk
- Coffee at Bar San Calisto
- Santa Maria in Trastevere church

**Lunch:**
- Trastevere (Da Enzo, Tonnarello)

**Afternoon:**
- Villa Borghese Gardens
  - Rent a bike or rowboat
  - If art lover: Borghese Gallery (book ahead!)
- Explore Parioli or Flaminio

**Evening:**
- Aperitivo in Testaccio or Ostiense
- Dinner wherever you haven't tried
- Gelato walk through centro

## Seasonal Adjustments

### Summer (Jun-Aug)
- Start EARLY (8am attractions)
- Siesta 1-5pm (too hot, things closed)
- Evening activities after 6pm
- Hydrate constantly
- August: expect closures

### Spring (Apr-May) & Fall (Sep-Oct)
- Perfect conditions
- Still crowded but manageable
- All outdoor activities good
- Book everything anyway

### Winter (Dec-Feb)
- Shorter lines
- Some sites close early
- Christmas markets (Dec)
- Rain expected — bring layers
- Great for museums

### Holy Week / Easter
- Extremely crowded
- Book months ahead
- Papal events if interested
- Beautiful but challenging
