# Where to Stay — Rome

## Best Areas by Traveler Type

| Type | Best Area | Why | Budget/Night |
|------|-----------|-----|--------------|
| First-timer | Centro Storico | Walking distance to everything | EUR 150-300 |
| Budget traveler | Termini/Esquilino | Hostels, transport hub | EUR 60-120 |
| Romantic getaway | Trastevere | Charming, restaurants | EUR 120-250 |
| Families | Prati | Quiet, near Vatican | EUR 100-200 |
| Luxury | Via Veneto / Piazza di Spagna | Historic luxury hotels | EUR 400+ |
| Long-term | Testaccio / Monteverde | Local feel, apartments | EUR 80-150 |

---

## Centro Storico (Historic Center)

### Why Stay Here
- Walk to Pantheon, Piazza Navona, Trevi
- Most beautiful area
- Restaurants, bars, atmosphere

### The Reality
- Expensive
- Crowded and noisy
- Often no elevator (old buildings)
- Cobblestones with luggage = hard

### Options
| Type | Price Range | Examples |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Luxury | EUR 400-800 | Hotel de Russie, Portrait Roma |
| Mid-range | EUR 150-300 | Boutique hotels, B&Bs |
| Budget | EUR 80-150 | Small hotels, guesthouses |

### Best Sub-Areas
- **Near Pantheon**: Central, beautiful, very touristy
- **Campo de' Fiori**: Market, nightlife, character
- **Piazza Navona area**: Beautiful, expensive
- **Near Trevi**: Convenient but VERY crowded

---

## Trastevere

### Why Stay Here
- Most atmospheric neighborhood
- Great restaurants and bars
- Local feel with tourist amenities
- Beautiful evening strolls

### The Reality
- Noisy at night (bars)
- 15-20 min walk to major sites
- Hilly cobblestones
- Limited hotel options (more B&Bs/apartments)

### Options
| Type | Price Range | Examples |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Mid-range | EUR 120-250 | Hotel Santa Maria, B&Bs |
| Budget | EUR 70-120 | Guesthouses, apartments |

### Best For
- Couples, foodies, people who want character over convenience

---

## Prati / Vatican Area

### Why Stay Here
- Near Vatican (walk)
- Quieter, residential feel
- Good restaurants (less touristy)
- Well-connected by metro

### The Reality
- Not "charming Rome"
- 20-30 min walk to centro
- More residential architecture
- Some areas boring

### Options
| Type | Price Range | Examples |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Mid-range | EUR 100-200 | Many 3-4 star hotels |
| Budget | EUR 70-120 | B&Bs, small hotels |

### Best For
- Families, Vatican-focused visitors, people wanting quiet nights

---

## Termini / Esquilino

### Why Stay Here
- Transport hub (trains, metro, buses)
- Cheapest area for hotels/hostels
- Many options

### The Reality
- Not charming (some say ugly)
- Can feel sketchy at night
- Immigrant neighborhood (diverse, some tensions)
- Budget = compromise on atmosphere

### Options
| Type | Price Range | Examples |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Budget hotel | EUR 70-120 | Chain hotels, basic |
| Hostel | EUR 25-60 | The Beehive, Yellow Hostel |

### Best For
- Budget travelers, early train departures, people who just need a bed

### Warning
Stay closer to Via Nazionale or Monti side, not east of Termini station.

---

## Monti

### Why Stay Here
- Trendy, hip neighborhood
- Near Colosseum
- Boutiques, wine bars, cafes
- More local than centro

### The Reality
- Increasingly popular = expensive
- Limited hotel options
- Can be noisy (nightlife)

### Options
| Type | Price Range | Examples |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Boutique | EUR 150-250 | Small design hotels |
| B&B | EUR 100-180 | Many options |

### Best For
- Hip travelers, couples, people wanting trendy + central

---

## Testaccio

### Why Stay Here
- Best food neighborhood
- Authentic Roman feel
- Nightlife
- Less touristy

### The Reality
- 20-30 min from major sites
- Limited lodging options
- Requires more local knowledge
- Better for longer stays

### Options
- Mostly apartments and B&Bs
- EUR 80-150 range

### Best For
- Foodies, longer stays, repeat visitors

---

## Airbnb / Apartments

### Pros
- Kitchen (save money)
- More space
- Local neighborhoods
- Better for groups/families

### Cons
- No daily housekeeping
- Check-in can be complicated
- Quality varies
- Regulations changing (some illegal listings)

### Best For Apartments
- Trastevere (character)
- Testaccio (authentic)
- Prati (families)
- Centro (convenience)

### What to Check
- Reviews (obviously)
- Elevator (old buildings often don't have)
- AC (essential May-September)
- Wifi quality
- Actual location (verify address)

## Budget Strategies

### Cheapest Options
1. **Hostels near Termini** (EUR 25-50)
2. **B&Bs in outer neighborhoods** (EUR 60-90)
3. **Airbnb in Garbatella/Testaccio** (EUR 70-100)
4. **Religious guesthouses** (EUR 50-80, some convents accept guests)

### Money-Saving Tips
- **Avoid Easter/Christmas** — Prices double
- **August** — Cheaper but hot, many things closed
- **Book 4+ weeks ahead** for best rates
- **Apartments with kitchen** — Cook breakfast, picnic lunches
- **Off-center** — 15-min metro ride saves EUR 50+/night

## Event Periods (Book Early, Expect High Prices)

| Event | When | Impact |
|-------|------|--------|
| Easter / Holy Week | March-April | Very high prices, very crowded |
| Christmas / New Year | Dec 24 - Jan 2 | High prices |
| August 15 (Ferragosto) | Mid-August | Many locals gone, some closures |
| Rome Marathon | March | Some hotels fill |
| Fashion weeks | Various | Via Veneto area |

## Transport from Hotels

### Centro Storico
- Walk to most sites
- Metro is 10-15 min walk
- Bus stops nearby
- Taxi for Vatican

### Trastevere
- Walk to centro (20 min)
- Tram 8 to centro
- Bus to Vatican

### Prati
- Walk to Vatican (5-15 min)
- Metro A to centro
- Bus options

### Termini
- Metro hub (A + B lines)
- All buses
- Trains
- Most connected area

---

## Accessibility Notes

- Rome is NOT accessible-friendly
- Cobblestones everywhere
- Many buildings without elevators
- Metro has some accessible stations
- Specify needs when booking
- Taxis and some buses are options
