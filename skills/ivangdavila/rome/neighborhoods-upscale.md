# Upscale Neighborhoods

## Parioli

### Overview
- **Vibe**: Wealthy residential, diplomatic, green
- **Rent 1BR**: EUR 1,500-2,500/month
- **Walk Score**: 75

### Who Lives Here
- Wealthy Roman families
- Diplomats and embassy staff
- International school families
- Executives
- Some older expats

### Pros
- Very safe
- Villa Borghese adjacent (park)
- International schools
- Quiet residential feel
- Good restaurants (upscale)
- Metro access

### Cons
- Far from centro (20-30 min)
- Can feel boring/suburban
- Expensive
- Not very "Roman" feeling
- Limited nightlife

### Key Areas
- **Via Parioli**: Main street
- **Villa Ada adjacent**: Near the park
- **Piazza Ungheria**: Commercial center

### Best For
Families with children, diplomats, executives, people wanting safety + space.

## Prati

### Overview
- **Vibe**: Professional, Vatican-adjacent, organized
- **Rent 1BR**: EUR 1,100-1,500/month
- **Walk Score**: 85

### Who Lives Here
- Middle-upper class Romans
- Vatican workers
- Lawyers/professionals
- Families
- Some expats (quieter types)

### Pros
- Near Vatican (10-15 min walk)
- Metro access (Ottaviano, Lepanto)
- Organized street grid (unusual for Rome)
- Good shopping (Cola di Rienzo)
- Relatively quiet
- Better value than centro

### Cons
- Can feel sterile
- Less "character" than Trastevere/Monti
- Touristy near Vatican
- Evening quiet

### Key Streets
- Via Cola di Rienzo (shopping)
- Via Ottaviano (Vatican tourists)
- Piazza Mazzini (residential)

### Best For
Professionals, families, people wanting quiet + accessibility.

---

## Aventino

### Overview
- **Vibe**: Exclusive hilltop, peaceful, residential
- **Rent 1BR**: EUR 1,400-2,200/month
- **Walk Score**: 70

### Who Lives Here
- Wealthy Romans
- Diplomats (FAO nearby)
- Some expats seeking quiet
- Retirees

### Pros
- Beautiful and peaceful
- Orange Garden (best sunset views)
- Aventine Keyhole attraction
- Safe and quiet
- Near Testaccio food scene

### Cons
- Hilly (can be tiring)
- Limited services/shops
- Far from metro
- Very quiet (boring for some)
- Limited rentals available

### Best For
Retirees, diplomats, people seeking peace + beauty over convenience.

---

## Trieste-Salario

### Overview
- **Vibe**: Upper-middle class, residential, practical
- **Rent 1BR**: EUR 1,000-1,400/month
- **Walk Score**: 75

### Who Lives Here
- Professional families
- Some academics (near university)
- Middle-class Romans
- Practical expats

### Pros
- More affordable than Parioli
- Good transport links
- Villa Ada park nearby
- Real Roman neighborhood
- Markets and shops

### Cons
- Not charming or trendy
- Far from tourist sites
- Boring for younger people
- Some areas less maintained

### Best For
Practical long-term residents, families on budget, academics.

---

## Monteverde

### Overview
- **Vibe**: Green, family-oriented, middle-class
- **Rent 1BR**: EUR 900-1,300/month
- **Walk Score**: 70

### Who Lives Here
- Families with children
- Middle-class Romans
- Some creative types
- People seeking green space

### Sub-Areas
- **Monteverde Vecchio**: Older, more character
- **Monteverde Nuovo**: Newer, more suburban

### Pros
- Villa Pamphili (huge park)
- Family-friendly
- Reasonable prices
- Safe
- Good schools

### Cons
- Far from center (25-35 min)
- Limited nightlife
- Hilly
- Need bus to center

### Best For
Families, nature lovers, people prioritizing green space.

---

## Via Veneto / Ludovisi

### Overview
- **Vibe**: Historic glamour, embassy row, hotels
- **Rent 1BR**: EUR 1,800-3,000/month
- **Walk Score**: 90

### Who Lives Here
- Wealthy internationals
- Embassy staff
- Hotel guests (many hotels)
- Older wealthy Romans

### History
- 1960s dolce vita scene (Fellini)
- Has declined from peak glamour
- Still prestigious address

### Pros
- Near Villa Borghese
- Central location
- Prestigious
- Safe
- Beautiful buildings

### Cons
- Very expensive
- Can feel sterile/touristy
- Limited authentic neighborhood feel
- Many hotels, few residents

### Best For
Short luxury stays, diplomatic postings, nostalgic glamour-seekers.
