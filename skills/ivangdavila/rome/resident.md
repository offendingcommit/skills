# Living in Rome — Resident Guide

## Making Rome Home

Moving to Rome is more than logistics — it's adapting to a different rhythm of life. This guide covers what long-term residents need to know.

---

## The First Steps

### Essential Checklist (First Month)

**Legal/Admin**
- [ ] Codice Fiscale (tax code)
- [ ] Permesso di Soggiorno (if non-EU)
- [ ] Register residenza (after finding permanent housing)
- [ ] SSN registration (healthcare)
- [ ] Choose Medico di base (GP)
- [ ] Bank account

**Practical**
- [ ] Phone/SIM card
- [ ] Housing (temporary then permanent)
- [ ] Learn neighborhood basics
- [ ] Figure out transport
- [ ] Find essential shops

### Timeline Reality
- First 3 months: Survival mode
- 3-6 months: Getting comfortable
- 6-12 months: Feeling settled
- 1-2 years: Integration begins
- 2+ years: Rome becomes home

## Housing Long-Term

### Rental Market

**Contratto Types**
- **4+4**: Standard, 8 years total with protections
- **3+2 (Cedolare secca)**: Tax benefits for landlord, common
- **Transitorio**: 1-18 months, for temporary residents

**Finding Apartments**
- Immobiliare.it, Idealista, Casa.it
- Facebook groups (Rome Expats, etc.)
- Word of mouth
- Agencies (charge fees)

**Typical Requirements**
- 2-3 months deposit
- Proof of income
- Codice fiscale
- Valid documents
- Sometimes guarantor

**What to Check**
- Contract is registered (registered = protection)
- Utilities transfer process
- Building rules (condominio)
- Heating type and cost
- Natural light
- Noise levels

### Buying Property

**Process Overview**
1. Find property
2. Make offer (proposta d'acquisto)
3. Preliminary contract (compromesso)
4. Due diligence
5. Final deed (rogito) at notary
6. Registration

**Costs**
- Property price
- Notary: 1-2.5%
- Registration tax: 2-9% (varies)
- Agency: 3-4%
- Total: ~10-15% on top of price

**Considerations**
- Mortgage possible for foreigners (harder)
- Renovation common (old buildings)
- Building health (condominium finances)
- Legal checks essential
- Use a geometra or architect

## Daily Life Setup

### Utilities

**Electricity (Luce)**
- Main providers: Enel, Eni, Edison, A2A
- Market liberalized (compare prices)
- Voltura (transfer to your name)

**Gas**
- Often bundled with electricity
- Central heating or individual
- Bills can be high in winter

**Water (Acqua)**
- ACEA in Rome
- Usually in building fees or separate
- Less than other utilities

**Internet**
- TIM, Vodafone, Fastweb, WindTre, Iliad
- Fiber available in most areas
- EUR 25-35/month
- Contract often 24 months

**TV License (Canone RAI)**
- EUR ~90/year
- Charged through electricity bill
- Legal but controversial
- Exemptions for some

### Banking

**Opening Account**
- Codice Fiscale required
- Residenza helpful
- ID/passport
- Proof of address

**Common Banks**
- Intesa Sanpaolo
- UniCredit
- BNL
- Fineco (online)
- N26, Revolut (digital)

**What to Know**
- Many fees (account, cards, operations)
- Online banks cheaper
- SEPA transfers easy
- International transfers more complex

### Mobile Phone

**Providers**
- TIM, Vodafone, WindTre (big three)
- Iliad (disruptive, cheap)
- Virtual: Fastweb, PosteMobile

**Costs**
- EUR 5-15/month typical
- Data usually included
- Prepaid (ricaricabile) common
- Contract (abbonamento) available

**Setup**
- Buy SIM at shop
- Need ID and Codice Fiscale
- Activation quick
- Port number from other Italian carrier easy

## Work & Finances

### Taxes

**Fiscal Residency**
- 183+ days in Italy = fiscal resident
- Worldwide income taxable
- Tax treaties may apply

**Income Tax (IRPEF)**
- Progressive rates: 23-43%
- Plus regional and municipal additions
- Deductions available

**Commercialista**
- Italian accountant
- Essential for non-standard situations
- Cost: EUR 50-300/month
- Handles tax filings, compliance

### Working Options
- Employment (see business.md)
- Freelance/Partita IVA
- Remote for foreign company
- Self-employment

## Practical Rhythms

### Weekly Routine
- **Monday**: Everything reopening, some museums closed
- **Tuesday-Friday**: Normal business
- **Saturday**: Shopping, leisure
- **Sunday**: Family time, many things closed

### Annual Rhythm
- **September**: Rientro, everything restarts
- **October-December**: Cultural season
- **December**: Holiday slowdown
- **January-February**: Quiet period
- **March-May**: Beautiful weather
- **June**: End of school season
- **July-August**: Slowdown, August exodus
- **August 15 (Ferragosto)**: Everything stops

### Shopping
- **Supermarkets**: Conad, Carrefour, Coop, Lidl
- **Markets**: Various neighborhoods (Testaccio, Trionfale)
- **Small shops**: Specialty items, relationships
- **Sunday**: Limited shopping (some open)

## Leaving Rome

### If You Leave

**Admin Tasks**
- Cancel residenza
- Close utilities
- End contracts properly
- Bank account (can keep)
- SSN notification
- Tax situation (commercialista advice)

**Notice Periods**
- Housing: Per contract (often 6 months)
- Employment: Per contract
- Utilities: 30 days typical
