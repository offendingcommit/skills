# Coffee & Aperitivo Culture — Rome

## Coffee (Caffè)

### The Basics

**Types of Coffee:**
| Order | What You Get |
|-------|--------------|
| Caffè | Espresso (the default) |
| Caffè doppio | Double espresso |
| Caffè lungo | Long espresso (more water) |
| Caffè ristretto | Short espresso (concentrated) |
| Caffè macchiato | Espresso with milk spot |
| Cappuccino | Espresso + steamed milk + foam |
| Caffè latte | Espresso with lots of milk |
| Caffè corretto | Espresso "corrected" with alcohol |
| Caffè americano | Diluted espresso (tourist order) |
| Caffè shakerato | Shaken iced espresso (summer) |

### The Rules

**Standing vs Sitting:**
- **Standing at bar**: EUR 1-1.50 espresso
- **Sitting at table**: EUR 3-5+ (significant markup)
- **Piazza seating**: Even more (location premium)
- Most Romans stand — quick, cheap, social

**Cappuccino Timing:**
- **Before 11am**: Normal
- **After 11am**: Tourists do it, Italians don't
- **After meals**: Never (milk = heavy)
- Will they judge you? No. But they'll know you're foreign.

**The Ritual:**
1. Enter bar
2. Order at counter (if no cashier) or pay first
3. Leave receipt on counter
4. Drink standing
5. Leave (maybe small tip in jar)

### Best Coffee Bars

**Historic:**
- **Sant'Eustachio Il Caffè**: Near Pantheon, famous
- **Tazza d'Oro**: Pantheon area, granita di caffè
- **Antico Caffè Greco**: Via Condotti, historic (expensive sit-down)

**Specialty/Third Wave:**
- **Faro**: Specialty coffee, rare in Rome
- **Pergamino**: Specialty
- **Roscioli Caffè**: Quality

**Local Favorites:**
- Any neighborhood bar without tourists
- Baristas know regulars
- Community hub

## Aperitivo

### The Ritual

**What It Is:**
- Pre-dinner drinks with snacks
- Social tradition
- Bridge between work and dinner
- 6pm-9pm typically

**What You Get:**
- One drink (EUR 8-15)
- Snacks included (varies widely)
- Some places: full buffet
- Some: just chips and olives

### Types of Aperitivo

**Basic:**
- Drink + bowl of chips/nuts
- EUR 6-10
- Standard bars

**Buffet:**
- Drink + access to buffet
- EUR 10-15
- Can be a light dinner
- Quality varies wildly

**Upscale:**
- Craft cocktails + curated snacks
- EUR 12-20
- Better ingredients, smaller portions

### Classic Aperitivo Drinks

**Spritz:**
- Prosecco + bitter (Aperol, Campari, Select)
- Orange colored, refreshing
- Most popular order

**Negroni:**
- Gin + Campari + sweet vermouth
- Stronger, bitter
- Very Roman

**Americano:**
- Campari + sweet vermouth + soda
- Lighter than Negroni
- Refreshing

**Prosecco:**
- Sparkling wine
- Simple, festive

**Aperol/Campari Soda:**
- Pre-mixed, lighter
- Very casual

### Best Aperitivo Spots

**With View:**
- Hotel rooftop bars (expensive but views)
- Terrazza Caffarelli (Capitoline Museums)
- Aroma (near Colosseum)

**In Neighborhoods:**

**Trastevere:**
- Freni e Frizioni (buffet, crowded)
- Ma Che Siete Venuti a Fà (craft beer)
- Bar San Calisto (very local)

**Monti:**
- Ai Tre Scalini (wine focused)
- La Barrique (natural wine)
- Gatsby Café

**Testaccio:**
- Rec 23 (buffet)
- Oasi della Birra (beer)
- Various local bars

**Campo de' Fiori:**
- Open Bar (casual)
- Many options
- Tourist-heavy but convenient

## Wine Culture

### Wine with Meals

**House Wine (Vino della Casa):**
- Perfectly acceptable
- Usually local (Castelli Romani)
- EUR 8-15 per liter
- Red (rosso), white (bianco), rosé (rosato)

**Local Wines:**
- **Frascati**: White, local, light
- **Cesanese**: Red, from Lazio
- **Est! Est!! Est!!!**: White, amusing name
- **Castelli Romani**: Various from hills near Rome

**National Favorites:**
- Montepulciano (Abruzzo)
- Chianti (Tuscany)
- Barolo/Barbaresco (Piedmont) — expensive

### Wine Bars (Enoteche)

**Good Wine Bars:**
- Il Goccetto (Centro)
- Roscioli (also restaurant)
- Cul de Sac (Piazza Navona area)
- Buccone (Prati)
- Litro (Monteverde)

## Digestivo

### After Dinner Drinks

**Grappa:**
- Grape pomace distillate
- Strong, burns
- End of meal classic

**Amaro:**
- Bitter herbal liqueur
- Digestive aid
- Many varieties (Averna, Montenegro, Fernet)

**Limoncello:**
- Lemon liqueur
- Southern Italy origin
- Sweet, often complimentary

**When:**
- After dessert/coffee
- Sometimes offered free
- Helps digestion (supposedly)

## Practical Tips

### At Coffee Bars
- Cash often preferred
- Stand for speed and savings
- One euro tip in jar is generous
- Say "prego" after receiving

### At Aperitivo
- Arrive early (6-7pm) for best buffet
- One drink minimum usually
- Standing outside is part of it
- Spritz is safe default order

### At Wine Bars
- Ask for recommendations
- Try local before famous names
- By glass (calice) or bottle (bottiglia)
- Cheese/salumi pair well
