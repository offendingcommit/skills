# Local Knowledge — Rome

## How Romans Live

### Daily Rhythm

**Morning**
- Coffee at bar (standing, quick)
- Cornetto (croissant) optional
- Work by 9am
- Schools start 8-8:30am

**Midday**
- Lunch 1-2:30pm (still important)
- Some shops close (1-4pm or 1-5pm)
- Not everyone takes siesta, but many shops do

**Afternoon**
- Shops reopen 4-5pm
- Aperitivo 6-8pm (sacred)
- Evening strolling (passeggiata)

**Evening**
- Dinner 8-10pm
- Nightlife varies by age/area
- Weekends later

### The Weekend

**Saturday**
- Shopping (Via del Corso, Cola di Rienzo)
- Markets (Porta Portese on Sunday actually)
- Lunch out
- Aperitivo
- Late dinner

**Sunday**
- Family lunch (sacred)
- Markets (Porta Portese flea market)
- Strolling
- Most shops closed
- Some restaurants closed for dinner

## Cultural Codes

### Greetings
- **Buongiorno**: Good morning/day (until 4-5pm)
- **Buonasera**: Good evening (after 4-5pm)
- **Ciao**: Only with people you know well
- **Two kisses**: Common greeting (right cheek first usually)

### Dress
- Romans dress well, even casually
- No flip-flops outside beach
- Covered shoulders/knees for churches
- Athleisure less common than US
- Effort appreciated

### Dining
- Cappuccino only before 11am
- No pasta as side dish
- No parmesan on seafood pasta
- Bread is for "scarpetta" (wiping plate)
- Dinner before 8pm = clearly tourist

### Coffee
- Quick, standing
- One euro, done
- Not to-go culture
- Sitting costs more (coperto)

### Public Behavior
- Voice volume: moderate (not loud Americans)
- PDA: normal (this is Italy)
- Queuing: less rigid than UK/Germany
- Patience required (nothing is fast)

## Bureaucracy Survival

### The Reality
Italian bureaucracy is **legendary** for complexity. Patience is essential.

### Key Concepts
- **Codice Fiscale**: Tax code (need for everything)
- **Permesso di Soggiorno**: Residence permit
- **Residenza**: Official residence registration
- **Questura**: Immigration police
- **Comune**: Municipality
- **Agenzia delle Entrate**: Tax office

### Survival Tips
- Bring ALL documents (and copies)
- Arrive early
- Expect multiple visits
- Find someone who speaks Italian
- CAF offices help foreigners
- Patience, patience, patience

### Opening Hours
- Government offices: Often mornings only
- Lunch closures: 1-4pm common
- Some only 2-3 days per week
- Check before going

## Money Matters

### Cash vs Card
- Cash still important
- Many small places cash-only
- Cards increasingly accepted
- Keep small bills

### Tipping
- Not obligatory
- Round up or 5-10% if excellent
- Never 20% like USA
- Coperto is not a tip

### Haggling
- Not typical in shops
- Markets: sometimes possible
- Antique/flea markets: expected

### Scams
- Check restaurant bills carefully
- Insist on taxi meter
- Avoid tourist trap restaurants
- Count change

## Seasons of Rome

### Summer (June-August)
- Many Romans leave
- Some restaurants closed
- Beaches accessible
- Very hot
- Estate Romana events

### Ferragosto (August 15)
- EVERYTHING closes
- Whole week around it quiet
- Romans at beach
- Plan ahead

### September
- "Rientro" — everyone returns
- Back to school
- Cultural season starts
- City fully alive again

### Christmas
- Nativity scenes (presepi)
- Markets (not like German ones)
- Family focus
- Some closures Dec 25-26

## Roman Quirks

### Things That Close
- Museums: Usually Monday
- Restaurants: Some Sunday dinner, Monday
- Churches: Midday (12-3pm often)
- Shops: Sunday, Monday morning
- August: Everything possible

### Things That Run Late
- Buses
- Trains (sometimes)
- Dinner reservations (your side)
- Appointments
- Everything bureaucratic

### Things Romans Love
- Calcio (soccer) — Roma vs Lazio
- Food debates (which neighborhood best)
- Complaining about tourists
- Complaining about Rome
- Being Roman (deeply proud)

### Things Romans Do
- Double parking (everywhere)
- Talking loudly with hands
- Multi-generational family events
- Sunday pranzo (lunch)
- Aperitivo (daily for many)
