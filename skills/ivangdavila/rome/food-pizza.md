# Pizza & Street Food — Rome

## Roman Pizza Styles

### Pizza al Taglio (By the Cut)
**The Roman fast food**

- **What**: Rectangular pizza, sold by weight
- **Crust**: Thick, airy, crispy bottom
- **How to order**: Point at what you want, indicate size
- **Price**: EUR 2-5 per piece depending on size/toppings
- **Eat**: Standing, walking, or at counter

**Best pizza al taglio:**
- **Pizzarium (Bonci)**: The master, worth the trip to Prati
- **Antico Forno Roscioli**: Historic, excellent
- **Forno Campo de' Fiori**: Tourist-friendly but good
- **Supplì Roma**: Multiple locations

### Pizza Tonda (Round Pizza)
**Sit-down pizzeria style**

- **Crust**: Thin, crispy, almost cracker-like
- **Size**: Individual, one per person
- **Comparison**: Thinner than Neapolitan
- **Style**: Less charred, more crispy

**Best pizza tonda:**
- **Da Remo (Testaccio)**: Institution, outdoor chaos
- **Ai Marmi (Trastevere)**: Called "l'obitorio" (morgue) for marble tables
- **Emma**: Modern, Ghetto area
- **Pizzeria Formula Uno**: Classic, no frills

### Pizza al Taglio vs Tonda

| Aspect | Al Taglio | Tonda |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Shape | Rectangular | Round |
| Ordering | By weight/piece | Whole pizza |
| Crust | Thick, airy | Thin, crispy |
| Eating | Standing/walking | Seated |
| Meal type | Snack/quick lunch | Dinner |
| Price | EUR 2-6/piece | EUR 8-15/pizza |

## Classic Roman Toppings

### Traditional Options
- **Margherita**: Tomato, mozzarella, basil (the standard test)
- **Marinara**: Tomato, garlic, oregano (no cheese)
- **Capricciosa**: Artichokes, olives, ham, mushrooms
- **Diavola**: Spicy salami
- **Funghi**: Mushrooms
- **Quattro Stagioni**: Four sections, different toppings
- **Patate**: Potatoes (yes, on pizza — it's good)

### Roman Specials
- **Fiori di zucca**: Zucchini flowers, mozzarella, anchovy
- **Carciofi**: Artichokes
- **Porchetta**: Slow-roasted pork
- **Supplì on side**: Always get a supplì

### Not Roman
- Hawaiian (pineapple) — exists but not Italian
- Deep dish — Chicago
- Calzone — Neapolitan

## Supplì

### What It Is
- Fried rice ball with tomato ragù and mozzarella inside
- "Supplì al telefono" — cheese stretches like phone cord
- Roman street food staple
- Found at every pizza al taglio shop

### How to Order
- Just say "un supplì" at the counter
- EUR 1.50-2.50 typically
- Eat immediately (best hot)
- Often sold at pizza places

### Variations
- Classic (ragù + mozzarella)
- Cacio e pepe (trendy modern)
- Amatriciana style
- Carbonara style

### Best Supplì
- Supplì Roma
- Pizzarium
- Antico Forno Roscioli
- Any good pizza al taglio

## Other Street Food

### Porchetta
- Slow-roasted whole pig
- Crispy skin, tender meat
- Often in sandwich (panino con porchetta)
- Originally from Ariccia (Castelli Romani)

**Where:**
- Testaccio Market
- Er Buchetto (near Termini)
- Street markets

### Trapizzino
- Pizza pocket with fillings
- Invented in Rome (2008)
- Triangle shape
- Fillings: chicken cacciatora, oxtail, etc.

**Where:**
- Trapizzino (original, Testaccio and others)

### Pane e Panelle
- Chickpea fritters
- Originally Sicilian but found in Rome
- Light, crispy

### Filetti di Baccalà
- Fried salt cod
- Traditional Roman
- Crispy batter

**Where:**
- Dar Filettaro (Campo de' Fiori) — standing only

## Neapolitan vs Roman

| Aspect | Naples | Rome |
|--------|--------|------|
| Crust | Soft, charred, puffy | Thin, crispy |
| Center | Soupy | Firm |
| Size | Smaller | Larger |
| Edge | Cornicione (thick) | Minimal |
| Eating | Fold | Knife and fork |
| Style | Wood-fired | Often electric |

### In Rome
- Both styles available
- Traditional Roman is tonda (thin)
- Some Neapolitan-style places (Seu Pizza Illuminati)
- No wrong choice

## Ordering Tips

### At Pizza al Taglio
1. Point at what you want
2. Indicate size (use hands, or say "così" — this much)
3. They'll cut, weigh, tell you price
4. Say if you want it heated (scaldare)
5. Pay at register

### At Sit-Down Pizzeria
1. One pizza per person (normal)
2. Splitting is possible but unusual
3. Order supplì or fritti to start
4. Beer is typical (not wine with pizza)
5. Expect shared tables at busy places

### Prices
- Pizza al taglio: EUR 2-6/piece
- Sit-down pizza: EUR 8-15
- Supplì: EUR 1.50-2.50
- Beer with pizza: EUR 4-6

## When to Eat Pizza

### Lunch
- Pizza al taglio perfect
- Quick, cheap, satisfying
- Worker's lunch

### Dinner
- Sit-down pizzeria
- Social occasion
- Usually after 8pm

### Late Night
- Many pizzerias open until 12-1am
- Post-drinking food
- Formula Uno, others

### NOT Breakfast
- Italians don't do breakfast pizza
- (Tourists sometimes do)
