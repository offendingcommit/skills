# Tech Scene — Rome

## Overview

Rome's tech scene is **smaller than Milan** but growing. Government, enterprise, and heritage tech dominate. Startups exist but ecosystem less developed than Northern Italy.

### Reality Check
- Milan is Italy's tech capital
- Rome has government/institutional tech
- Startup scene exists but smaller
- Heritage/tourism tech opportunities
- Growing digital nomad presence

---

## Startup Ecosystem

### Reality
- Smaller than Milan
- Less VC activity
- More government/EU grants
- Heritage/tourism focus possible

### Incubators & Accelerators
- **Luiss EnLabs**: University-linked
- **DigitalMagics**: Italian network
- **Startupbootcamp**: Some programs
- **Impact Hub Rome**: Social innovation

### Funding Landscape
- Less VC than Milan
- Government grants (Invitalia, regional)
- EU programs (Horizon Europe)
- Angel investors exist but scarce
- Often need Milan connections

### Notable Startups (Rome-Based)
- Scene is smaller
- Many successful ones relocate to Milan
- Heritage/tourism tech opportunities
- GovTech opportunities

## Tech Jobs

### Job Market
- Fewer startup jobs than Milan
- More enterprise/consulting
- Government IT contracts
- Remote work for foreign companies

### Average Salaries (Gross Annual)

| Role | Junior | Mid | Senior |
|------|--------|-----|--------|
| Developer | EUR 25-35k | EUR 35-50k | EUR 50-70k |
| Data Scientist | EUR 30-40k | EUR 45-60k | EUR 60-80k |
| Product Manager | EUR 35-45k | EUR 50-65k | EUR 70-90k |
| Designer | EUR 25-35k | EUR 35-50k | EUR 50-70k |

**Notes:**
- Lower than Northern Europe
- Benefits often included
- 13th/14th month salary common
- Remote may pay more (foreign companies)

### Where to Find Jobs
- LinkedIn (standard)
- Indeed Italia
- Monster Italia
- Glassdoor
- Company websites
- Networking essential

## Digital Infrastructure

### Internet
- Fiber available in most areas (FTTH/FTTC)
- Major providers: TIM, Vodafone, Fastweb, Wind Tre
- Speed: 100-1000 Mbps possible
- Some older buildings limited

### Mobile
- 4G ubiquitous
- 5G expanding
- Major carriers: TIM, Vodafone, Wind Tre, Iliad
- Prepaid SIM cheap (EUR 5-15/month)

### Coworking Connectivity
- Generally reliable
- Check speed before committing
- Backup options good (cafés, etc.)

## Remote Work / Digital Nomad

### Working Remotely in Rome
- Growing DN presence
- Cafés less laptop-friendly than US
- Coworking is the norm
- Apartments with good wifi findable
- Time zone: CET (good for Europe, challenging for US West)

### Best Areas for Remote Work
- Monti (cafés, spaces)
- Pigneto (creative spaces)
- EUR (if you like corporate)
- Prati (professional)

### Practical Considerations
- Italian keyboard layout
- Electrical adapters needed
- Backup internet (mobile data)
- Video call background (beautiful!)

## Challenges

### Why Not Rome for Tech?
- Smaller ecosystem than Milan
- Less VC funding
- Brain drain to Milan, abroad
- Bureaucracy challenges
- Lower salaries

### Why Rome for Tech?
- Lower cost than Milan
- Quality of life
- Government/institutional opportunities
- Heritage/tourism niche
- Growing remote work scene
- Beauty and culture

## Recommendations

### For Developers
- Remote work for foreign companies viable
- Local market limited but exists
- Network through meetups
- Consider Milan for pure tech career

### For Entrepreneurs
- GovTech opportunities
- Heritage/tourism tech niche
- EU grants accessible

### For Digital Nomads
