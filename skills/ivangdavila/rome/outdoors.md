# Outdoors & Nature — Rome

## Urban Green Spaces

### Major Parks

**Villa Borghese**
- Rome's Central Park
- 80 hectares
- Near Spagna/Flaminio
- Bike/boat rentals
- Museums inside
- Best for: families, jogging, picnics

**Villa Ada**
- Second largest park
- 160+ hectares
- Parioli area
- Wilder, more natural
- Lake, running paths
- Best for: running, nature walks

**Villa Doria Pamphilj**
- Largest park (184 hectares)
- Monteverde area
- Less central but beautiful
- Running circuits marked
- Best for: long runs, escape from crowds

**Villa Torlonia**
- Nomentano area
- Museums (Mussolini's former residence)
- Nice gardens
- Best for: families, culture + nature

**Villa Celimontana**
- Near Colosseum
- Smaller, charming
- Jazz festival (summer)
- Best for: quick escape

**Parco degli Acquedotti**
- Aqueduct ruins
- Cinecittà area
- Dramatic scenery
- Best for: photography, picnics

### Gardens

**Giardino degli Aranci (Orange Garden)**
- Aventine Hill
- Rome views
- Small but perfect
- Sunset essential
- Best for: sunset, romance

**Orto Botanico**
- Trastevere
- Botanical gardens
- University-run
- Peaceful
- Best for: plant lovers, quiet

## Running

### Popular Routes

**Villa Borghese Circuit**
- ~5km loop
- Mostly flat
- Traffic-free
- Beautiful scenery
- Best for: central, easy

**Villa Doria Pamphilj**
- Various loops (5-15km)
- Marked circuits
- Hilly terrain
- Serious runners
- Best for: longer runs

**Lungotevere (River)**
- Along the Tiber
- Flat, urban
- Night running possible
- Best for: convenient

**Villa Ada**
- Natural terrain
- 5-10km options
- Less crowded
- Best for: trail-like running

**Parco degli Acquedotti**
- Flat, scenic
- Ancient aqueducts
- Early morning best
- Best for: something different

### Running Groups
- Rome Hash House Harriers
- Atleticom running groups
- Parkrun (various locations)
- Running stores organize group runs

### Tips
- Early morning best (heat, crowds)
- Summer: before 8am or after 8pm
- Carry water
- Cobblestones in centro (careful)

## Cycling

### In the City

**Reality Check**
- Rome is NOT bike-friendly
- Traffic aggressive
- Cobblestones challenging
- Some bike lanes exist
- Improving slowly

**Bikeable Areas**
- Villa Borghese (inside park)
- Villa Doria Pamphilj
- Lungotevere (some sections)
- EUR (flat, wide streets)

### Bike Sharing
- Various apps (Lime, Bird, etc.)
- E-scooters more common
- Quality varies
- Helmets not required but recommended

### Bike Rental
- Shops near parks
- EUR 10-20/day
- E-bikes available

### Best Cycling Experience

**Via Appia Antica**
- Ancient Roman road
- Traffic-limited (Sundays closed to cars)
- Rent bikes at start
- 15+ km route
- Catacombs along way
- Best day trip cycling

## Day Trips for Nature

### Within 1 Hour

**Castelli Romani**
- Hill towns south of Rome
- Lakes (Albano, Nemi)
- Forests, walking trails
- Wine and porchetta
- Train accessible

**Tivoli**
- Villa d'Este gardens
- Hadrian's Villa
- Not wilderness but beautiful
- 45 min by train/bus

**Bracciano Lake**
- Swimming (summer)
- Castle town
- 1 hour north
- Train accessible

### 1-2 Hours

**Monte Terminillo**
- Mountains near Rieti
- Hiking in summer
- Skiing in winter
- 2 hours by car

**Parco Nazionale del Circeo**
- Coastal national park
- Beaches, dunes, forests
- 1.5 hours south
- Car recommended

**Sabina Hills**
- Rolling countryside
- Olive groves
- Small villages
- Agriturismo stays

### Beach Options

**Ostia**
- Closest (30 min train)
- Free beaches and stabilimenti
- Not beautiful but accessible
- Crowded summer

**Sperlonga**
- Beautiful, 1.5 hours south
- Blue water, cliffs
- Worth the trip
- Train + bus or car

**Santa Marinella**
- 45 min train
- Decent beaches
- Day trip easy
- Crowded weekends

## Seasonal Considerations

### Spring (Mar-May)
- Perfect for everything
- Wildflowers in parks
- Not too hot
- Some rain

### Summer (Jun-Aug)
- Very hot
- Early morning or evening only
- Beach season
- Mountains cooler

### Fall (Sep-Nov)
- Excellent hiking weather
- Swimming still possible (Sept)
- Beautiful colors
- Some rain

### Winter (Dec-Feb)
- Mild for walking
- Skiing in mountains
- Parks still nice
- Rain more likely
