# Food by Neighborhood — Rome

## Testaccio — The Food Capital

### Why Here
- Former slaughterhouse district
- Workers paid in offal → quinto quarto tradition
- Testaccio Market (must visit)
- Least touristy of the food neighborhoods

### Must-Try Restaurants

**Felice a Testaccio**
- Famous for cacio e pepe and carbonara
- Reservations essential
- Old-school Roman
- EUR 30-45/person

**Flavio al Velavevodetto**
- All Roman classics done well
- Cave-like setting (ancient pottery mountain)
- Consistent quality
- EUR 25-35/person

**Checchino dal 1887**
- Invented coda alla vaccinara (oxtail)
- Best for offal dishes
- Historic, elegant
- EUR 50-70/person

**Da Remo**
- Best pizza in Testaccio
- Outdoor tables, chaotic
- Cash only, no reservations
- EUR 10-15/person

### Testaccio Market
- Indoor market, food stalls
- Mordi e Vai (porchetta sandwiches)
- Fresh produce, cheese, meat
- Lunch destination

## Trastevere — Character + Cuisine

### Why Here
- Most atmospheric neighborhood
- Cobblestone streets, ivy walls
- Mix of tourist and local spots
- Evening destination

### Must-Try Restaurants

**Da Enzo al 29**
- Possibly best traditional Roman
- Tiny, always full
- Book days ahead
- EUR 25-35/person

**Tonnarello**
- Famous cacio e pepe
- Large portions
- More touristy but good
- EUR 20-30/person

**Nannarella**
- Local favorite
- Less discovered
- Excellent quality
- EUR 25-35/person

**Grazia & Graziella**
- Romantic setting
- Good traditional
- Nice wine list
- EUR 35-45/person

### Avoid
- Restaurants with staff outside pulling you in
- Picture menus
- "Menu turistico" signs
- Main piazza (Santa Maria) tourist traps

---

## Jewish Ghetto — Historic Cuisine

### Why Here
- Oldest Jewish community in Europe (since 161 BC)
- Jewish-Roman cuisine fusion
- Famous for fried artichokes
- Historic significance

### Must-Try Restaurants

**Nonna Betta**
- Best carciofi alla giudia
- Traditional Jewish-Roman
- Cozy atmosphere
- EUR 30-40/person

**Ba'Ghetto**
- Kosher option available
- Traditional dishes
- Good all-around
- EUR 35-45/person

**Piperno**
- Historic (1856)
- Upscale setting
- All classics
- EUR 50-70/person

### Signature Dishes
- Carciofi alla giudia (fried artichokes)
- Fiori di zucca (fried zucchini flowers)
- Baccalà (salt cod, fried)
- Straccetti di manzo (beef strips)

## Centro Storico — Convenience + Traps

### The Reality
- Most convenient location
- Most tourist traps
- Some excellent spots hidden among traps
- Requires knowing where to go

### Worth It

**Armando al Pantheon**
- Steps from Pantheon
- Excellent traditional
- Reservations essential
- EUR 40-50/person

**Roscioli**
- Salumeria + restaurant
- Wine selection excellent
- Carbonara famous
- EUR 50-70/person

**Pierluigi**
- Seafood focus
- Beautiful piazza
- Upscale
- EUR 60-80/person

### Avoid
- Anything on Piazza Navona
- Via del Corso restaurants
- Picture menus, tourist menus
- Aggressive hawkers

---

## Monti — Trendy + Wine

### Why Here
- Hip neighborhood
- Wine bars and boutiques
- Less chaotic than Trastevere
- Near Colosseum

### Must-Try

**Ai Tre Scalini**
- Wine bar + food
- Great cheese/salumi
- Local crowd
- EUR 20-30/person

**Urbana 47**
- Modern Roman
- Breakfast to dinner
- Creative but grounded
- EUR 30-40/person

**La Barrique**
- Natural wine
- Light food
- Trendy
- EUR 25-35/person

### Best For
- Wine lovers
- Lighter meals
- Aperitivo to dinner

---

## Prati — Vatican Adjacent

### Why Here
- Near Vatican
- Less touristy than Vatican entrance area
- Good local options
- Organized streets

### Must-Try

**Pizzarium (Bonci)**
- Best pizza al taglio in Rome
- Worth the trip
- Gourmet slices
- EUR 10-15/person

**Sciascia Caffè**
- Historic café
- Near Castel Sant'Angelo
- Good pastries
- EUR 5-10

**Del Frate**
- Good traditional
- Local clientele
- Reasonable
- EUR 25-35/person

### Avoid
- Anything immediately at Vatican entrance
- Via della Conciliazione restaurants

---

## San Lorenzo — Student Budget

### Why Here
- University area
- Cheap eats
- Alternative vibe
- Less polished

### Options
- Pizza places (cheap, casual)
- Ethnic food (diverse options)
- Student bars with food
- Budget-friendly trattorias

### Best For
- Budget eating
- Late night
- Young crowd
- Casual atmosphere

---

## Pigneto — Hipster Dining

### Why Here
- Emerging food scene
- Creative restaurants
- Alternative vibe
- Less tourists

### Worth Exploring
- Necci dal 1924 (historic bar-restaurant)
- Various new openings
- International options
- Craft cocktail bars
