# Visitor Tips — Rome

## Before You Arrive

### Best Time to Visit
| Season | Weather | Crowds | Prices | Verdict |
|--------|---------|--------|--------|---------|
| Spring (Apr-May) | Perfect 18-25C | High | High | Best weather, worth crowds |
| Summer (Jun-Aug) | Hot 30-38C | Extreme | High | Avoid if possible |
| Fall (Sep-Oct) | Perfect 18-25C | Moderate | Moderate | Best overall |
| Winter (Dec-Feb) | Cool 8-15C | Low | Low | Great value, some rain |

### What to Pack
- **Comfortable walking shoes** — Cobblestones everywhere
- **Layers** — Churches cold, outside hot
- **Scarf/cover-up** — For church dress codes
- **Power adapter** — European Type C/L plugs
- **Portable charger** — Long days out
- **Light rain jacket** — Weather changes fast

### Booking Ahead (Essential)
- **Vatican Museums**: 2-4 weeks ahead minimum
- **Colosseum**: 1-2 weeks ahead
- **Borghese Gallery**: 2+ weeks ahead (mandatory)
- **Popular restaurants**: 1-3 days ahead
- **Opera/concerts**: Weeks ahead

## Getting Around

### From the Airport

**Fiumicino (FCO)** — Main airport
| Mode | Cost | Time |
|------|------|------|
| Leonardo Express train | EUR 14 | 32 min to Termini |
| Regional train FL1 | EUR 8 | 45+ min |
| Bus (SIT, Terravision) | EUR 6-8 | 45-60 min |
| Taxi (fixed fare) | EUR 50 | 45-60 min |
| Uber/Bolt | EUR 40-60 | 45-60 min |

**Ciampino (CIA)** — Budget airlines
| Mode | Cost | Time |
|------|------|------|
| Bus to Termini | EUR 6 | 40-60 min |
| Taxi | EUR 31 (fixed) | 30-40 min |

### Within Rome
- **Metro**: 3 lines, covers main areas, EUR 1.50
- **Bus**: Extensive but chaotic, same ticket
- **Tram**: Several lines, scenic
- **Walking**: Best for centro storico
- **Taxi**: White official taxis only, meter or fixed fare

### Day Passes
| Type | Cost | Best For |
|------|------|----------|
| Single (100 min) | EUR 1.50 | Occasional use |
| 24-hour | EUR 7 | Heavy transit day |
| 48-hour | EUR 12.50 | 2-day intensive |
| 72-hour | EUR 18 | 3-day intensive |
| 7-day (CIS) | EUR 24 | Week stay |

### Taxi Tips
- Only WHITE licensed taxis
- Insist on meter OR agree fixed price first
- Fixed fares: Airport EUR 50, Termini-Vatican ~EUR 15
- Night/Sunday surcharge applies
- No tipping expected (round up if you want)

## Money & Payments

### Currency
- **Euro (EUR)**
- Cash still common (have small bills)
- Cards accepted most places (not everywhere)
- Some small shops/trattorias cash-only

### Tipping
- **Not obligatory** — Service often included
- **Restaurants**: Round up or 5-10% if excellent
- **Bars**: EUR 0.20-0.50 at counter, more if seated
- **Taxis**: Round up
- **Hotels**: EUR 1-2/bag for porter

### Coperto (Cover Charge)
- EUR 1-3/person added to restaurant bills
- This is normal and legal
- Covers bread, table setting
- Not a tip

## Food & Drink Tips

### Eating Like a Roman
- **Colazione (breakfast)**: Coffee + cornetto at bar, standing
- **Pranzo (lunch)**: Main meal, 12:30-2:30pm
- **Aperitivo (pre-dinner)**: 6-8pm, drinks + snacks
- **Cena (dinner)**: 8-10pm (9pm+ more local)

### Coffee Rules
- **Espresso**: Just say "caffè"
- **Standing at bar**: Cheapest
- **Sitting at table**: 2-3x more expensive (cover charge)
- **Cappuccino**: Only before 11am (local rule)
- **No to-go cups**: Drink at bar

### Restaurant Red Flags
- Picture menus on the sidewalk
- "Menu turistico"
- Hawkers pulling you in
- Near major monuments
- English-only menus (some exceptions)

### Restaurant Green Flags
- Handwritten daily specials
- Locals eating there
- Off main tourist streets
- Specific Roman dishes
- No high chairs (often means authentic)

## Safety

### Generally Safe But Watch For
- **Pickpockets**: Metro, crowded sites, bus 64
- **Scams**: Gladiators wanting photo money, rose sellers, fake petitions
- **Bag snatching**: Rare but keep bags secure
- **ATM skimmers**: Use bank ATMs

### Safety Tips
- Use money belt or front pocket
- Don't leave bags on chairs
- Be aware in crowds
- Avoid empty metro cars late night
- Termini area sketchy after dark

### Emergency Numbers
- **112**: General emergency (English available)
- **113**: Police
- **118**: Medical emergency
- **115**: Fire

## Cultural Etiquette

### Church Dress Codes
- **Covered shoulders** (no tank tops)
- **Covered knees** (no shorts above knee)
- **Applies to everyone** — Men and women
- **Bring a scarf** — Can cover shoulders
- **You will be turned away** if not dressed properly

### Greetings
- **Buongiorno**: Until 4-5pm
- **Buonasera**: After 4-5pm
- **Ciao**: Only with people you know well
- **Two kisses**: Common greeting (cheeks, not lips)

### Dining Etiquette
- Wait to be seated
- Don't ask for Parmesan on seafood pasta
- Don't ask for butter with bread
- Don't ask for chicken on pasta
- Bread is for wiping plate (fare la scarpetta)
- Espresso after dessert, not with

### Siesta Hours
- **1pm-4pm**: Many shops close
- **Sunday**: Most non-tourist shops closed
- **Monday morning**: Many museums closed
- **August**: Many businesses closed

## Day Trips from Rome

### Within 1 Hour
| Destination | Train Time | Why Go |
|-------------|------------|--------|
| Tivoli | 45-60 min | Villa d'Este, Hadrian's Villa |
| Ostia Antica | 30 min | Roman ruins, fewer crowds |
| Castelli Romani | 30-45 min | Hill towns, wine, porchetta |
| Orvieto | 70 min | Duomo, wine, cliff town |

### 1-2 Hours
| Destination | Train Time | Why Go |
|-------------|------------|--------|
| Florence | 90 min (fast) | Art, architecture |
| Naples | 70 min (fast) | Pizza, chaos, Vesuvius |
| Pompeii | 2 hours | Ancient ruins |
| Assisi | 2 hours | St. Francis, peaceful |

### Day Trip Tips
- Book fast train (Frecciarossa) in advance for deals
- Start early (7-8am trains)
- Return by 7-8pm
- Italo and Trenitalia are train operators

## Common Mistakes

### Don't
- Eat near the main monuments
- Sit on the Spanish Steps
- Expect things to be efficient
- Try to see everything
- Eat dinner before 8pm
- Stand on the left on escalators
- Forget to validate train tickets
- Assume English is spoken everywhere

### Do
- Book major sites in advance
- Carry cash
- Dress modestly for churches
- Take siesta (or at least rest)
- Eat where locals eat
- Learn basic Italian phrases
- Walk as much as possible
- Accept the chaos and enjoy it
