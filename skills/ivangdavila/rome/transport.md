# Transport — Rome

## From the Airports

### Fiumicino (FCO) — Main Airport

**Leonardo Express (Best for most)**
- Direct train to Termini
- EUR 14 one-way
- 32 minutes
- Every 15 minutes
- Buy at station or online

**FL1 Regional Train**
- EUR 8 to Trastevere, Ostiense, Tiburtina
- 45+ minutes
- Good if not going to Termini
- More stops

**Bus (SIT, Terravision, etc.)**
- EUR 6-8 to Termini
- 45-60 minutes (traffic dependent)
- Cheapest option
- Book online

**Taxi (Fixed Fare)**
- EUR 50 fixed to anywhere in Aurelian Walls
- 45-60 minutes (traffic)
- Official white taxis only
- Agree on price first

**Uber/Bolt**
- Available, similar to taxi
- EUR 40-60 typically
- App-based convenience

### Ciampino (CIA) — Budget Airlines

**Bus to Termini**
- EUR 6 various companies
- 40-60 minutes
- Most common option

**Taxi**
- EUR 31 fixed to center
- 30-40 minutes

## Metro (Metropolitana)

### Lines
- **Line A (Red)**: Battistini ↔ Anagnina
  - Key stops: Ottaviano (Vatican), Spagna, Termini
- **Line B (Blue)**: Laurentina ↔ Rebibbia/Jonio
  - Key stops: Colosseo, Termini, Piramide, EUR
- **Line C (Green)**: Monte Compatri ↔ San Giovanni
  - Newest, still expanding
  - Connects to A and B at San Giovanni

### Operating Hours
- **First train**: ~5:30am
- **Last train**: 23:30 (Sun-Thu), 1:30am (Fri-Sat)
- Check for strikes (sciopero)

### Tickets
| Type | Price | Validity |
|------|-------|----------|
| BIT (single) | EUR 1.50 | 100 minutes, 1 metro ride |
| 24-hour | EUR 7 | Unlimited |
| 48-hour | EUR 12.50 | Unlimited |
| 72-hour | EUR 18 | Unlimited |
| CIS (7-day) | EUR 24 | Unlimited |

**Buy at**: Metro stations, tabacchi, newsstands
**Validate**: Stamp at turnstile/machine

### Tips
- Rush hour: 8-9am, 5-7pm (crowded)
- Pickpockets active
- Centro Storico has no metro (walk or bus)
- Air conditioning exists (usually)

## Buses & Trams

### Bus Network
- Extensive but confusing
- Same tickets as metro
- Enter front/back, validate middle
- Display shows next stops

### Key Routes
- **64**: Termini → Vatican (pickpocket express)
- **40**: Termini → Vatican (express, fewer stops)
- **H**: Termini → Trastevere
- **8 (Tram)**: Trastevere → Centro

### How to Use
1. Find stop (fermata)
2. Check route number
3. Wait (schedules approximate)
4. Board, validate ticket
5. Exit from middle/back doors

### Night Buses
- After metro closes
- "N" prefix (N1, N2, etc.)
- Less frequent
- Check routes carefully

## Taxis

### Official Taxis
- White only
- "TAXI" light on roof
- Meter or fixed fare
- License visible

### Getting One
- Taxi stands (posteggio taxi)
- Call: 060609 (Rome number)
- Apps: FreeNow, itTaxi
- Hotels can call
- Street hailing possible

### Fares
- **Minimum**: EUR 3 (EUR 6.50 Sundays/holidays)
- **Per km**: EUR 1.10 (city), EUR 1.50 (out)
- **Waiting**: EUR 27/hour
- **Luggage**: EUR 1/bag
- **Night (10pm-6am)**: Surcharge

### Fixed Fares
- FCO Airport ↔ Center: EUR 50
- Ciampino ↔ Center: EUR 31
- Termini ↔ Vatican: ~EUR 15

### Tips
- No tipping required (round up if you want)
- Insist on meter or agree price FIRST
- Receipt available (ricevuta)
- Only official white taxis

## Walking

### The Best Option for Centro

- Most of Centro Storico is pedestrianized or limited traffic
- Colosseum to Vatican: ~45 min walk
- Pantheon to Trastevere: ~20 min
- Comfortable shoes essential (cobblestones!)

### Walking Tips
- Cobblestones everywhere
- Hills in some areas (Trastevere, Aventino)
- Cross streets carefully (drivers aggressive)
- Google Maps/Citymapper work well
- Hydrate in summer

## Bikes & Scooters

### Bike Sharing
- Various apps available
- Challenging terrain (hills, cobblestones)
- Traffic can be intimidating
- Villa Borghese good for cycling

### Electric Scooters
- Multiple apps (Lime, Bird, etc.)
- Popular with tourists
- Dangerous on cobblestones
- Helmet not required but recommended

### Renting
- Shops near major sites
- EUR 10-20/day for bike
- Consider terrain before committing

## Driving

### Don't

Seriously, avoid driving in Rome:
- Traffic chaotic
- Parking impossible
- ZTL zones (fines!)
- Public transport + walking sufficient

### If You Must

**ZTL (Limited Traffic Zone)**
- Centro Storico restricted
- Cameras catch violations
- Fines of EUR 100+ each entry
- Hotel may have exemption (ask first)

**Parking**
- Blue lines: Pay to park
- White lines: Free (rare)
- Yellow lines: Reserved
- Garage: Expensive but safe

## Accessibility

### Challenges
- Rome is NOT accessibility-friendly
- Cobblestones everywhere
- Many stations lack elevators
- Old buildings, no ramps
- Steps at many attractions

### What Works
- Some metro stations accessible (check map)
- Buses: Some with ramps
- Taxis: Can accommodate
- Major museums improving
- Vatican has accessibility route

### Resources
- Sage Traveling (accessibility guides)
- Contact attractions directly
- Book accessible taxis in advance
