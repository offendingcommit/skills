# Attractions — Rome

## Must-See (Worth the Hype)

### The Colosseum
- **What**: Iconic Roman amphitheater (70-80 AD)
- **Why go**: The symbol of Rome, genuinely impressive
- **When**: Early morning or late afternoon (skip midday heat/crowds)
- **Cost**: EUR 18 (includes Roman Forum and Palatine Hill)
- **Time needed**: 2-3 hours with Forum
- **Pro tip**: Book online weeks ahead, skip-the-line essential
- **Underground tours**: Extra cost but worth it

### Vatican Museums & Sistine Chapel
- **What**: One of world's greatest art collections
- **Why go**: Michelangelo's Sistine Chapel, Raphael Rooms
- **When**: Wednesday (Pope audience thins crowds), Friday evenings
- **Cost**: EUR 17-21
- **Time needed**: 3-4 hours minimum
- **Pro tip**: Book months ahead, or try Friday night opening
- **Skip if**: Severe crowd anxiety — it's packed

### St. Peter's Basilica
- **What**: Largest church in the world
- **Why go**: Michelangelo's Pieta, the dome, sheer scale
- **When**: Early morning (opens 7am)
- **Cost**: Free (dome climb EUR 8-10)
- **Time needed**: 1-2 hours (add 1 hour for dome)
- **Dress code**: Covered shoulders and knees required

### Roman Forum & Palatine Hill
- **What**: Heart of ancient Rome, imperial palaces
- **Why go**: Walk where Caesar walked, panoramic views
- **When**: Included with Colosseum ticket, do together
- **Cost**: Included with Colosseum (EUR 18 total)
- **Time needed**: 2-3 hours
- **Pro tip**: Enter from Palatine Hill for fewer crowds

### Pantheon
- **What**: Best-preserved Roman building (125 AD)
- **Why go**: The dome is engineering marvel, free, beautiful
- **When**: Anytime, but morning light through oculus is magical
- **Cost**: Free
- **Time needed**: 30-60 minutes
- **Pro tip**: Go when it rains — water through the oculus

### Trastevere
- **What**: Charming neighborhood across the Tiber
- **Why go**: Rome's most atmospheric quarter, food, nightlife
- **When**: Evening for dinner and strolling
- **Cost**: Free to walk
- **Time needed**: 2-4 hours
- **Best for**: Dinner, wine, people-watching

## Worth Your Time

### Piazza Navona
- **What**: Baroque square with Bernini fountains
- **Why go**: Beautiful, lively, quintessential Rome
- **When**: Evening for atmosphere
- **Cost**: Free
- **Time needed**: 30-60 minutes

### Trevi Fountain
- **What**: Baroque masterpiece, coin-throwing tradition
- **Why go**: Iconic, beautiful, romantic
- **When**: Very early morning or late night (crowds insane)
- **Cost**: Free (bring coins to throw)
- **Time needed**: 15-30 minutes
- **Warning**: Extremely crowded 10am-10pm

### Spanish Steps
- **What**: Famous stairway and piazza
- **Why go**: Iconic, good people-watching, shopping nearby
- **When**: Sunset for photos
- **Note**: Sitting on steps is now FINED
- **Time needed**: 15-30 minutes

### Castel Sant'Angelo
- **What**: Former papal fortress, now museum
- **Why go**: Great views, interesting history, near Vatican
- **When**: Late afternoon for sunset views
- **Cost**: EUR 15
- **Time needed**: 1-2 hours

### Borghese Gallery
- **What**: Art museum in villa, Bernini sculptures
- **Why go**: World-class art in intimate setting
- **When**: Timed entry only, book 2+ weeks ahead
- **Cost**: EUR 15
- **Time needed**: 2 hours (that's your time slot)
- **Pro tip**: Reservation MANDATORY, walk-ins impossible

### Villa Borghese Gardens
- **What**: Rome's Central Park
- **Why go**: Escape crowds, rent bikes, picnic
- **When**: Anytime, nice break from sightseeing
- **Cost**: Free (bike rental EUR 15/hour)
- **Time needed**: 1-3 hours

### Campo de' Fiori
- **What**: Market square, neighborhood
- **Why go**: Morning market, evening drinks
- **When**: Morning for market (Mon-Sat), evening for nightlife
- **Cost**: Free to walk
- **Time needed**: 1-2 hours

## Worth It If You Have Time

### Capitoline Museums
- **What**: World's oldest public museum
- **Why go**: Roman sculptures, great piazza designed by Michelangelo
- **Cost**: EUR 15
- **Time needed**: 2-3 hours

### Appian Way (Via Appia Antica)
- **What**: Ancient Roman road with catacombs
- **Why go**: Escape crowds, bike ancient road, catacombs
- **When**: Sunday (road closed to cars)
- **Cost**: Free (catacomb tours EUR 8-10)
- **Time needed**: Half day

### Testaccio
- **What**: Working-class neighborhood, food scene
- **Why go**: Authentic Roman food, local vibe
- **When**: Lunch or dinner
- **Time needed**: 2-3 hours

### Jewish Ghetto
- **What**: Historic Jewish quarter, oldest in Europe
- **Why go**: History, fried artichokes, atmosphere
- **Cost**: Free to walk
- **Time needed**: 1-2 hours

## Tourist Traps (Lower Priority)

### Hard Rock Cafe Rome
- **Reality**: Same as everywhere, not Roman
- **Instead**: Any local trattoria

### Termini Area Restaurants
- **Reality**: Tourist traps, bad food, high prices
- **Instead**: Walk 10 minutes in any direction

### Hop-on Hop-off Buses
- **Reality**: Rome is walkable, these waste time
- **Instead**: Walk, or use metro + walking

### Restaurants with Picture Menus on Via Veneto
- **Reality**: Tourist traps, overpriced mediocre food
- **Instead**: Testaccio, Trastevere, Jewish Ghetto

## Hidden Gems

### Aventine Keyhole
- **What**: Look through keyhole at Priory of Malta
- **Why go**: Perfect framed view of St. Peter's dome
- **When**: Early morning (no line)
- **Cost**: Free
- **Time needed**: 15 minutes

### Orange Garden (Giardino degli Aranci)
- **What**: Small park with Rome views
- **Why go**: Peaceful, romantic, great sunset
- **When**: Sunset
- **Cost**: Free

### Quartiere Coppede
- **What**: Art Nouveau neighborhood
- **Why go**: Unique architecture, fairy-tale feeling
- **When**: Anytime
- **Cost**: Free

### Non-Catholic Cemetery
- **What**: Cemetery where Keats and Shelley are buried
- **Why go**: Peaceful, beautiful, poetic
- **Cost**: Donation requested
- **Time needed**: 30-60 minutes

### Centrale Montemartini
- **What**: Ancient sculptures in old power plant
- **Why go**: Unique setting, no crowds
- **Cost**: EUR 10
- **Time needed**: 1-2 hours
