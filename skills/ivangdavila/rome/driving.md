# Driving in Rome

## The Reality

**Do you need a car in Rome?** Usually NO.

### When You Don't Need One
- Living in centro/central neighborhoods
- Daily life (metro, bus, walking)
- Tourist sites
- Most commutes

### When You Might Want One
- Families in suburban areas
- Frequent day trips
- Work outside city
- Specific lifestyle needs

---

## If You Drive

### License Requirements

**EU License**
- Valid in Italy indefinitely
- No conversion needed
- Register if becoming resident

**Non-EU License**
- Valid for 1 year with IDP
- IDP (International Driving Permit) from home country
- After 1 year: must convert to Italian

**Converting License**
- Motorizzazione Civile
- Varies by country (some require test)
- Bureaucratic process
- Allow 2-4 months

## ZTL — Zone a Traffico Limitato

### What It Is
- Limited Traffic Zones
- Centro Storico restricted
- Active certain hours
- Cameras catch violations
- Fines: EUR 80-100+ per entry

### Where
- Centro Storico (historic center)
- Trastevere (parts)
- Testaccio (parts)
- Vatican area (parts)
- Other neighborhoods

### When Active
- Varies by zone
- Typically: Mon-Fri 6:30-18:00
- Some: 24/7 Friday-Saturday night
- Check specific zones

### How to Know
- Signs at entry points
- Camera indicators
- "Varco attivo" = ZTL active
- Apps show zones (ATAC, maps)

### Exemptions
- Residents (permit required)
- Disabled (permit)
- Hotels can arrange temporary permits
- Rentals: Specify if entering ZTL

## Parking

### Types

**Blue Lines**
- Pay to park
- Parking meter nearby
- Apps: EasyPark, Telepass Pay
- EUR 1-3/hour typically
- Time limits often

**White Lines**
- Free parking
- Rare in center
- Time limits sometimes
- Check signs

**Yellow Lines**
- Reserved (disabled, residents, loading)
- Never park here

**Garage/Parcheggio**
- Indoor parking
- Expensive (EUR 2-5/hour, EUR 20-40/day)
- Safer for nice cars
- Near major sites

### Finding Parking
- Very difficult in center
- Apps help (Parkopedia, EasyPark)
- Consider park-and-ride
- Double parking is Roman tradition (don't)

## Getting a Car

### Buying

**New Car**
- Dealerships throughout
- Financing available
- Registration (immatricolazione)
- Insurance mandatory

**Used Car**
- Private sales
- Dealers
- AutoScout24, Subito.it
- Get mechanic check
- Passaggio di proprietà (ownership transfer)

### Registration
- At ACI or Motorizzazione
- Documents needed
- Cost: ~EUR 500+ for transfer
- License plate stays with car

### Insurance (Assicurazione)
- Mandatory (RC Auto)
- Compare: Facile.it, Segugio.it
- Cost: EUR 300-1,000+/year
- Depends on car, driver, history

## Renting a Car

### When to Rent
- Day trips
- Weekend escapes
- Temporary needs
- Testing before buying

### Companies
- Major: Hertz, Avis, Europcar, etc.
- Italian: Maggiore, Sicily By Car
- Budget: Locauto, Goldcar
- Book ahead for better rates

### Costs
- Small car: EUR 30-50/day
- Medium: EUR 50-80/day
- Weekend rates often better
- Insurance: Add significantly

### Tips
- Check ZTL policy with rental company
- Full insurance recommended
- Photo car before and after
- Small car better for Rome
- Manual transmission default

### Picking Up
- Airport locations convenient
- Fiumicino: Many options
- City locations exist
- Return: Same or different

## Driving Culture

### The Reality
- Aggressive driving
- Creative interpretation of rules
- Honking common
- Double parking endemic
- Scooters everywhere
- Pedestrians cross anywhere

### Survival Tips
- Be assertive but careful
- Expect the unexpected
- Watch for scooters
- Don't panic
- Use horn appropriately
- Smaller car = easier

### Common Issues
- Narrow streets
- One-ways everywhere
- Parking nightmares
- Traffic jams
- Road rage exists

## Fuel

### Types
- **Benzina**: Gasoline/petrol
- **Gasolio/Diesel**: Diesel
- **GPL**: LPG
- **Metano**: Natural gas

### Prices
- EUR 1.70-2.00/liter (varies)
- Self-service (fai da te) cheaper
- Full service (servito) available

### Stations
- ENI, IP, Q8, Esso, Tamoil
- Many self-service only at night
- Some accept only cash after hours
- Pay at machine or cashier

---

## Tolls

### Autostrada (Highway)
- Most highways tolled
- Take ticket at entry
- Pay at exit
- Cash or card
- Telepass for electronic payment

### Telepass
- Electronic toll device
- No stopping at booths
- Monthly fee
- Worth it if driving frequently
- Get at Autostrade points or online

## Fines & Penalties

### Common Fines
| Violation | Fine |
|-----------|------|
| ZTL entry | EUR 80-100 |
| Parking violation | EUR 40-100 |
| Speeding | EUR 40-500+ |
| Phone while driving | EUR 160-650 |
| Seatbelt | EUR 80+ |
| Red light | EUR 160+ |

### Cameras
- ZTL entries
- Speed cameras (autovelox)
- Red light cameras
- Some bus lanes

### If You Get a Fine
- Pay promptly for discount (often 30%)
- Contest within 60 days if disputing
- Rental cars: Company may charge admin fee
- Foreign fines do follow you (EU)

## Day Trip Driving

### Popular Destinations
| Destination | Distance | Time |
|-------------|----------|------|
| Tivoli | 30 km | 45 min |
| Castelli Romani | 25 km | 40 min |
| Orvieto | 120 km | 1.5 hr |
| Civita di Bagnoregio | 130 km | 2 hr |
| Sperlonga | 130 km | 1.5 hr |

### Tips
- Avoid rush hours (7-9am, 5-8pm)
- GRA (ring road) often congested
- Weekend exodus Friday evening
- Sunday return traffic Sunday evening
- GPS essential (Waze popular)

## Practical Advice

### Don't Drive If
- Staying in centro
- Short visit
- Anxiety about traffic
- Not essential

### Do Consider Driving For
- Families needing flexibility
- Living in suburbs
- Frequent countryside trips
- Specific work needs

### Alternatives
- Rent when needed
- Car sharing
- Train for day trips
- Uber/taxi for airport
