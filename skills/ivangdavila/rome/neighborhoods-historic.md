# Historic Center Neighborhoods

## Centro Storico

### Overview
- **Vibe**: Tourist heart, ancient monuments, expensive
- **Rent 1BR**: EUR 1,400-2,000/month
- **Walk Score**: 95

### Who Lives Here
- Short-term visitors (Airbnb dominant)
- Wealthy Italians (family apartments)
- Some expats with high budgets
- Very few "regular" residents left

### Key Areas
- **Pantheon area**: Most central, most touristy
- **Piazza Navona**: Beautiful, expensive
- **Campo de' Fiori**: Market, nightlife
- **Via del Corso**: Shopping, younger

### Pros
- Walk to everything
- Beautiful surroundings
- Restaurant/bar density
- No need for transport

### Cons
- Extremely touristy
- Very expensive
- Noisy (day and night)
- Few supermarkets
- Buildings old, no elevators often

### Best For
Short-term visitors, wealthy retirees, people prioritizing location over everything.

## Trastevere

### Overview
- **Vibe**: Charming, nightlife, expat favorite
- **Rent 1BR**: EUR 1,200-1,800/month
- **Walk Score**: 90

### Who Lives Here
- Expats and digital nomads (largest community)
- Students
- Young professionals
- Some Roman families (longtime residents)
- Artists and creatives

### Pros
- Most character of any neighborhood
- Best restaurant scene
- Vibrant nightlife
- Strong expat community
- 15-20 min walk to centro
- Less touristy than centro (barely)

### Cons
- VERY noisy at night (especially weekends)
- Hilly, cobblestones
- Getting expensive
- Touristy in main areas
- Limited parking

### Sub-Areas
- **Main Trastevere**: Around Santa Maria, most lively
- **Upper Trastevere**: Quieter, near Gianicolo hill
- **Near Porta Portese**: More local, market area

### Best For
Young expats, digital nomads, foodies, people wanting character + community.

---

## Monti

### Overview
- **Vibe**: Trendy, boutique, gentrified
- **Rent 1BR**: EUR 1,200-1,700/month
- **Walk Score**: 90

### Who Lives Here
- Young professionals
- Creative industry workers
- Fashion/design people
- Trendy expats
- Wealthy young Romans

### Pros
- Near Colosseum (5 min walk)
- Boutique shops, wine bars
- Less chaotic than Trastevere
- Metro access (Cavour)
- Trendy without being touristy

### Cons
- Getting expensive quickly
- Small apartments
- Limited nightlife (wine bars, not clubs)
- Some tourist overflow

### Key Streets
- Via del Boschetto (shopping)
- Via dei Serpenti (cafes)
- Via Panisperna (quieter)

### Best For
Trendy expats, creative professionals, people wanting hip + central.

## Campo de' Fiori & Jewish Ghetto

### Overview
- **Vibe**: Historic market, ancient Jewish quarter
- **Rent 1BR**: EUR 1,300-1,900/month
- **Walk Score**: 95

### Who Lives Here
- Mix of tourists and locals
- Some longtime Roman families
- Expats seeking central location

### Campo de' Fiori
- Morning market (Mon-Sat)
- Evening drinking scene
- Young crowd
- Noisy at night

### Jewish Ghetto
- Oldest Jewish community in Europe
- Incredible food (fried artichokes!)
- More residential feel
- Historic significance
- Quieter than Campo

### Pros
- Central location
- Market access
- Historical significance
- Good food scene

### Cons
- Noisy (Campo especially)
- Touristy
- Expensive
- Limited supermarkets

### Best For
Short-term stays, history buffs, market lovers.

---

## San Lorenzo

### Overview
- **Vibe**: Student quarter, alternative, affordable
- **Rent 1BR**: EUR 800-1,200/month
- **Walk Score**: 80

### Who Lives Here
- University students (La Sapienza nearby)
- Young creatives
- Artists
- Budget expats

### Pros
- Cheapest central-ish area
- Young, vibrant energy
- Good nightlife (student bars)
- Near Termini station
- Authentic, not touristy

### Cons
- Can feel rough/edgy
- Graffiti heavy
- Less "pretty" than other areas
- Some safety concerns at night

### Best For
Students, young budget travelers, people seeking alternative vibe.
