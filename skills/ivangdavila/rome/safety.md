# Safety — Rome

## Overall Assessment

Rome is **generally safe** — much safer than many major cities. Violent crime is rare. Your main concerns are **pickpockets** and **scams**, not physical danger.

### Safety by Category

| Category | Level | Notes |
|----------|-------|-------|
| Violent crime | Very Low | Rare, especially for tourists |
| Pickpockets | Moderate-High | Main risk, especially at sites |
| Scams | Moderate | Tourist-targeted, avoidable |
| Women alone | Generally Safe | Normal urban precautions |
| Night safety | Good | Most areas safe, some exceptions |
| Natural disasters | Low | Occasional minor earthquakes |

## Pickpockets

### High-Risk Areas
- **Metro Line A**: Especially Termini-Spagna stretch
- **Bus 64**: "Pickpocket express" to Vatican
- **Colosseum area**: Crowded tourist zone
- **Vatican entrance**: Crowds, distraction
- **Trevi Fountain**: Very crowded
- **Termini Station**: Transit hub chaos

### How They Operate
- **Distraction**: One person distracts, another steals
- **Crowding**: Push in crowds
- **Bumping**: "Accidental" contact
- **Fake petitions**: Hold clipboard, steal while you're distracted
- **Rose sellers**: Push flower, grab during confusion
- **Kids with cardboard**: Cover bag while taking items

### Prevention
- **Money belt**: For passport, large cash
- **Front pocket**: For daily spending
- **Cross-body bag**: Harder to snatch
- **Zippered pockets**: Extra barrier
- **Awareness**: Look up, not at phone
- **Split resources**: Don't keep everything together
- **Hotel safe**: Leave passport copy, extra cards

## Common Scams

### The Gladiators
- **What**: Men in costumes offer photos
- **Scam**: Demand EUR 20-50 after photo
- **Avoid**: Don't engage, walk away

### Rose Sellers
- **What**: Push roses at couples
- **Scam**: Demand payment, distraction theft
- **Avoid**: Firm "no," don't touch

### Petition Signers
- **What**: Clipboard, ask to sign
- **Scam**: Steal while you're distracted
- **Avoid**: Ignore completely, keep walking

### Friendship Bracelets
- **What**: Tie bracelet on wrist
- **Scam**: Demand payment, aggressive
- **Avoid**: Don't extend arm, walk away

### Taxi Overcharging
- **What**: No meter, inflated price
- **Prevent**: White taxis only, insist on meter or fixed fare
- **Fixed fares**: FCO airport EUR 50, Ciampino EUR 31

### Restaurant Tourist Traps
- **What**: Overpriced, poor quality
- **Prevent**: Avoid picture menus, tourist menus, hawkers

## Women's Safety

### General Assessment
Rome is **reasonably safe** for women, including solo travelers. Italian culture involves more attention than Northern Europe, but harassment is typically verbal, not physical.

### What to Expect
- **Catcalling**: Can happen, especially solo
- **Staring**: More common than Northern Europe
- **Flirting**: Italian men may be forward
- **Physical harassment**: Rare

### Tips
- **Ignore**: Don't engage with unwanted attention
- **Confidence**: Walk purposefully
- **Night**: Stick to main streets, well-lit areas
- **Transport**: Sit near driver on empty buses
- **Termini area**: Avoid alone late at night
- **Trust instincts**: If uncomfortable, leave

### Reporting
- Emergency: 112
- Police (Carabinieri): 112
- Anti-violence hotline: 1522 (Italian)

## LGBTQ+ Safety

### General Climate
- Rome is **relatively tolerant** compared to smaller Italian cities
- Open displays of affection generally safe in center
- Pride parade annually
- Gay-friendly bars/areas exist (mainly Ostiense, San Giovanni)

### Cautions
- Some outer neighborhoods more conservative
- Smaller towns around Rome less accepting
- Older generations may be less tolerant
- Vatican is obviously conservative

### Resources
- Gay Village (summer event)
- Circolo Mario Mieli (LGBTQ+ organization)
- Coming Out bar (near Colosseum)

## Health & Medical Safety

### Emergency Number
- **112**: General emergency (English available)
- **118**: Medical emergency specifically

### Hospitals with English
- Rome American Hospital (private)
- Salvator Mundi International Hospital
- Public hospitals have some English (not guaranteed)

### Pharmacies (Farmacia)
- Green cross sign
- Minor ailments treatable
- Many speak some English
- Some open 24/7 (turno)

### Water
- **Tap water**: Safe to drink
- **Nasoni**: Public fountains (safe, free)
- **Restaurants**: Will charge for bottled

### Food Safety
- Generally high standards
- Watch for heat-exposed food in summer
- Restaurant hygiene usually good

## Natural Hazards

### Earthquakes
- Italy is seismically active
- Rome experiences minor tremors occasionally
- Major earthquakes rare in city
- Know building exits

### Heat
- Summer: 35°C+ possible
- Hydrate constantly
- Seek shade midday
- Sunscreen essential

### Air Quality
- Traffic pollution exists
- Generally acceptable
- Some summer smog
- Check AQI if sensitive

## Practical Security Tips

### Documents
- Carry photocopy, leave original in safe
- Digital copy in email/cloud
- Note passport number separately

### Money
- Split cash between locations
- Note card emergency numbers
- Inform bank of travel

### Phone
- Keep charged
- Know emergency numbers
- Download offline maps
- Consider data plan

### Evening Out
- Tell someone your plans
- Arrange safe transport home
- Watch drinks
- Stick to main areas

### General Awareness
- Look confident
- Stay aware (not paranoid)
- Trust your instincts
- Ask hotel staff for advice
