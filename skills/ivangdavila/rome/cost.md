# Cost of Living — Rome

## Overview

Rome is **moderately expensive** by European standards — cheaper than London, Paris, or Zurich, but more expensive than Lisbon, Barcelona, or Eastern European capitals.

### Monthly Budget Summary

| Lifestyle | Monthly Budget | Description |
|-----------|---------------|-------------|
| Survival | EUR 1,200-1,500 | Shared room, cook always, no dining out |
| Budget | EUR 1,500-2,000 | Shared apartment, occasional dining |
| Comfortable | EUR 2,000-3,000 | Own apartment, regular dining out |
| Comfortable+ | EUR 3,000-4,000 | Nice area, good lifestyle |
| Luxury | EUR 4,000+ | Prime location, no budget concerns |

## Money-Saving Tips

### Housing
- Look outside tourist center
- Shared apartments save significantly
- Negotiate on longer leases
- Facebook groups for deals

### Food
- Supermarket shopping vs restaurants
- Tavola calda for cheap lunches
- Aperitivo buffets can be dinner
- Markets for fresh produce

### Transport
- Monthly pass if using transit daily
- Walk — Rome is walkable
- Bike in flat areas

### Entertainment
- Free entry first Sunday of month (state museums)
- Roma Pass for multiple attractions
- Student discounts (bring ID)
- Free churches, piazzas, walking

### General
- Italian SIM card (very cheap)
- Avoid tourist trap restaurants
- Drink standing at bars
- Shop at local markets
