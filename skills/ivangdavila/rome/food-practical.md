# Practical Food Guide — Rome

## Reservations

### Always Reserve
- Da Enzo al 29 (days ahead)
- Armando al Pantheon
- Roscioli
- Felice a Testaccio
- Any popular spot on weekends

### Usually Walk-In OK
- Pizza al taglio (always)
- Tavola calda (cafeteria style)
- Weekday lunches
- Less famous trattorias
- Markets

### How to Book
- **Call**: Italian preferred, English usually OK
- **TheFork app**: Very common in Rome
- **Instagram DM**: Some modern places
- **Hotel concierge**: Can help

### Timing
- Book 2-3 days ahead for popular spots
- Weekend dinner: Book earlier
- Lunch: Often easier

## Meal Timing

### Breakfast (Colazione) 7-10am
- Quick at bar
- Espresso + cornetto (croissant)
- EUR 2-3 standing
- No big breakfasts

### Lunch (Pranzo) 12:30-2:30pm
- Traditional main meal
- Many restaurants close after
- Reopens around 7:30pm
- Tavola calda for quick option

### Aperitivo 6-9pm
- Pre-dinner drinks + snacks
- Can substitute light dinner
- Social ritual

### Dinner (Cena) 8-11pm
- **8pm**: Early (tourists)
- **9pm**: Normal
- **10pm**: Late but fine
- **Before 8pm**: Very unusual

### Sunday
- Big family lunch tradition
- Many restaurants closed Sunday dinner
- Some closed all Sunday
- Plan ahead

## Menu Navigation

### Structure
```
Antipasti → Starters
Primi → First courses (pasta, soup, rice)
Secondi → Main courses (meat, fish)
Contorni → Side dishes (ordered separately!)
Dolci → Desserts
```

### Ordering
- You don't HAVE to order every course
- Just primo is totally acceptable
- Secondo + contorno (sides separate)
- Ask for recommendations

### Common Menu Items
| Italian | English |
|---------|---------|
| del giorno | of the day |
| alla romana | Roman style |
| alla griglia | grilled |
| fritto | fried |
| al forno | baked |
| stagionale | seasonal |

## Prices & Payment

### Typical Costs
| Type | Per Person |
|------|------------|
| Coffee standing | EUR 1-1.50 |
| Coffee seated | EUR 3-5 |
| Pizza al taglio | EUR 3-6 |
| Tavola calda lunch | EUR 8-12 |
| Trattoria dinner | EUR 20-35 |
| Nice restaurant | EUR 40-60 |
| Fine dining | EUR 80+ |

### Coperto
- EUR 1-3 per person cover charge
- Includes bread, table
- Legal and normal
- Not a tip

### Servizio
- Sometimes added (10-15%)
- Check bill before tipping
- If included, no additional tip needed

### Tipping
- **Not obligatory** in Italy
- If no servizio: round up or 5-10%
- Never 20% like USA
- Cash tips preferred

### Payment
- Cash still common
- Cards increasingly accepted
- Small places may be cash only
- Ask if unsure ("Posso pagare con carta?")

## Dietary Requirements

### Vegetarian
- "Sono vegetariano/a"
- Possible but limited options
- Many pastas have guanciale (pork)
- Cacio e pepe is vegetarian
- Ask about ingredients
- Side dishes (contorni) available

### Vegan
- "Sono vegano/a"
- Challenging at traditional places
- Some dedicated vegan restaurants
- Pasta with vegetables possible
- Ask carefully about cheese, eggs

### Gluten-Free
- "Senza glutine"
- Challenging in pasta culture
- Some restaurants have GF pasta
- Pizza GF at some places
- Secondi usually safe
- Risotto options

### Allergies
- "Sono allergico/a a..."
- Taken seriously
- Nut allergies: careful with desserts
- Shellfish: clearly marked usually

### Kosher
- Jewish Ghetto has options
- Ba'Ghetto is certified
- Limited elsewhere

## Restaurant Types

### Ristorante
- Full service, formal
- Most expensive
- White tablecloths often
- Multi-course expected

### Trattoria
- Traditional, casual
- Family-run often
- Good value
- Core Roman food

### Osteria
- Wine-focused traditionally
- Now often like trattoria
- Good food + wine

### Tavola Calda
- Cafeteria style
- Point at what you want
- Workers' lunch
- Very cheap

### Pizzeria
- Pizza focus
- Casual, often noisy
- One pizza per person
- Beer traditional (not wine)

### Pizza al Taglio
- Slice shop
- Standing/walking
- By weight
- Snack or quick lunch

### Bar
- Coffee + pastry
- Some light food
- Standing cheaper
- Not American "bar"

### Enoteca
- Wine bar
- Light food (cheese, salumi)
- Wine focus

## Ordering Water

- **Acqua naturale**: Still water
- **Acqua frizzante**: Sparkling water
- **Del rubinetto**: Tap water (can ask, may get looks)
- Water usually EUR 2-3 per bottle

---

## Bread

- Comes automatically (part of coperto)
- Used for "scarpetta" (wiping plate)
- Butter not traditional
- Don't fill up on bread

## Red Flags Checklist

❌ Picture menus on sidewalk  
❌ Staff aggressively calling you in  
❌ "Tourist menu" signs  
❌ Empty at 9pm on Saturday  
❌ Near major monuments only  
❌ No Italians eating there  
❌ Frozen food indicators  
❌ Microwaved dishes  

✅ Handwritten specials  
✅ Small menu  
✅ Seasonal items  
✅ Locals dining  
✅ Busy at 9pm  
✅ Recommendations when asked  
✅ Off main tourist path
